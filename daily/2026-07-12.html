Apple sues OpenAI in Northern California, naming Chief Hardware Officer Tang Tan and engineer Chang Liu over trade-secret theft it says ran "at every level"

Apple's complaint accuses OpenAI's hardware chief Tang Tan, a former Apple vice president, of directing Apple candidates to bring "actual parts" to OpenAI interviews for show-and-tell, and accuses former Apple senior electrical engineer Chang Liu of skipping his exit interview, keeping his laptop, and exploiting a post-departure Apple cloud bug to download more than a thousand pages of confidential technical documents. The suit arrives four months after Apple confirmed the revamped Siri will run on Google Gemini instead of OpenAI's models, and Apple frames the two decisions as connected.

Business Today · 2026-07-11 · Source ↗ ·

Apple filed suit against OpenAI, its hardware subsidiary IO Products, and two named former Apple employees in the US District Court for the Northern District of California late Friday, and the complaint circulated through wire coverage into Saturday morning. Business Today's July 11 story is the most complete public account of what the filing actually says. The named individual defendants are Tang Tan, now Chief Hardware Officer at OpenAI and previously a vice president at Apple, and Chang Liu, a former senior electrical engineer at Apple. The complaint is the first time Apple has taken a US frontier-AI lab to court, and it lands four months after Apple confirmed the autumn 2026 revamp of Siri will run on Google's Gemini rather than OpenAI's models.

The two allegations doing the work in the filing are specific. Apple claims Tan directed Apple engineers interviewing at OpenAI to bring "actual parts" from Apple products to their interviews so he and his team could run "show and tell" sessions and elicit confidential Apple information from candidates during the interview loop. Apple frames those sessions as a targeted intelligence-gathering process rather than a normal hiring practice. The second allegation concerns Liu. Apple says Liu never returned his company laptop, skipped his exit interview, and then exploited a bug in Apple's internal cloud storage that allowed a departed employee to reach files he no longer had entitlement to. Apple says Liu used that opening to download dozens of confidential files including over a thousand pages of technical documents on his way out the door.

"OpenAI, through its hardware subsidiary IO Products, engaged in a scheme to acquire Apple's trade secrets at every level of its organization."— Apple complaint, N.D. Cal., filed July 10 2026

The IO Products backdrop

Apple's suit lands on top of OpenAI's $6.4 billion acquisition of IO Products, the Jony Ive-founded hardware venture OpenAI closed earlier this year to build a family of AI-native consumer devices. IO Products staffed heavily out of Apple's Cupertino design and hardware ranks. Apple's complaint reads the hiring pattern as more than incidental. It ties the show-and-tell allegation directly to IO Products' recruiting pipeline and argues that OpenAI's device roadmap depends on know-how developed on Apple's dime. The two named defendants sit at nodes Apple chose deliberately: Tan is the most senior former Apple executive at OpenAI, and Liu allegedly walked out with the paperwork.

OpenAI's response was brief. A spokesperson told the BBC the company is reviewing Apple's complaint and denied any interest in another company's trade secrets. Neither Tan nor Liu commented publicly on Saturday. Apple did not put a dollar figure on damages in the initial filing, but the complaint asks for injunctive relief on IO Products' hardware program and a full accounting of any documents Liu allegedly retained. Trade-secret cases of this shape are slow, expensive, and rarely produce the injunctions plaintiffs open with, but they generate discovery that runs through years of internal email at both companies.

The Siri-Gemini link

Business Today reports the case comes "just months after Apple confirmed it would build its revamped Siri assistant on Google's Gemini AI models instead of OpenAI's ChatGPT technology, ending speculation that ChatGPT would remain central to Apple Intelligence." That timeline stitches together. Apple picked Gemini in March. Apple filed against OpenAI in July. The Siri-Gemini switch is now the operational fact underneath the litigation, and it means Apple no longer has a direct commercial reason to keep the relationship civil. Whatever residual leverage OpenAI had via Siri distribution is gone; the litigation reads as Apple burning that bridge intentionally.

"We have no interest in other companies' trade secrets. We are reviewing Apple's complaint."— OpenAI spokesperson, statement to BBC, July 10 2026

What breaks next

Three immediate consequences. First, OpenAI's confidential S-1, filed June 8 for a targeted 2027 IPO, now carries a material disclosure item that has to appear in the amended registration. Trade-secret litigation with a Big Tech plaintiff and specific named executives is exactly the class of risk item underwriters push into the risk factors section. Second, IO Products' device roadmap is now on a clock. Discovery will surface which parts of the OpenAI hardware program relied on the allegedly appropriated material, and any injunctive risk on that program is what OpenAI actually cares about protecting. Third, this closes the era in which Big Tech and the frontier labs cooperated through commercial deals and cross-executive movement. The Apple–OpenAI partnership around ChatGPT-in-Siri was the standard bearer for that model. Apple's lawsuit against the same counterpart, over hires that supported the same product line, marks the point at which Apple stops treating OpenAI as a partner and starts treating it as a competitor to litigate against.

OpenAI publishes a claimed GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra proof of the 50-year-old Cycle Double Cover Conjecture generated in an hour by 64 coordinated subagents

Crypto Briefing · 2026-07-11 · Source ↗

The conjecture, posed by Szekeres in 1973 and Seymour in 1979, asks whether every bridgeless graph carries a family of cycles covering each edge exactly twice. OpenAI's Ethan Knight posted the proof PDF and the full prompt on X. GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra Mode coordinates 64 subagents that communicate mid-task rather than only handing off results. Peer review of the artifact starts now.

OpenAI made GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra generally available Thursday and on Friday morning Ethan Knight, on X, published a PDF the company says the model generated in under an hour by coordinating 64 subagents on a single problem: the Cycle Double Cover Conjecture. The conjecture, posed independently by Szekeres in 1973 and Seymour in 1979, asks whether every bridgeless graph admits a collection of cycles such that each edge appears in exactly two cycles. The problem has been a central open question in structural graph theory for roughly five decades, closely tied to nowhere-zero flow theorems and the four-colour theorem in a family of results Tutte and Younger sketched out in the 1960s. Multiple prior claimed proofs have been withdrawn after gaps surfaced.

The technical claim OpenAI is making has two layers. The first is the artifact itself, a full proof PDF and the exact prompt Knight used to generate it. The second is the process. Ultra Mode is not 64 copies of the same model running in parallel. It coordinates 64 subagents that are trained to communicate with each other during the run and revise each other's arguments mid-task rather than only combining outputs at the end. Crypto Briefing frames the run as an operational demonstration that mid-task communication is where the marginal gain sits on hard-mathematics problems where a single agent's reasoning trajectory stalls.

"Yesterday, we made GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra generally available. Today, we're sharing that it produced a proof of the 50-year-old Cycle Double Cover Conjecture using 64 subagents in just under one hour."— Ethan Knight, OpenAI, on X, July 10 2026

Peer review is the whole story now. The Cycle Double Cover Conjecture has attracted claimed proofs before, including several posted to arXiv that were later found to have gaps or were withdrawn under pressure from working combinatorialists. The mathematics community will need weeks at minimum to verify the argument, and probably months for any formal acceptance. What is different this time is the transparency of the artifact. OpenAI released both the prompt and the PDF, which lets independent researchers reproduce the generation and audit the reasoning trace step by step. If the proof holds, the model has produced a genuinely new theorem in structural graph theory. If it does not hold, the failure will still be diagnostic about where mid-task-communicating subagents run out of runway on hard problems.

Cloudflare turns on granular AI-crawler controls for every customer tier, splitting Search, Agent and Training with a September 15 block-by-default flip for ad-supported pages

Cloudflare Developers · 2026-07-11 · Source ↗

Search covers indexing crawlers that return referral traffic. Agent covers real-time browser-use and chat-fetch bots acting on behalf of a user. Training covers fine-tuning corpora crawlers. Multi-purpose crawlers such as Googlebot, Applebot and Bingbot inherit the most restrictive policy. On September 15 all new domains onboarded to Cloudflare default to block Agent and Training on ad-supported pages.

Cloudflare turned on the granular AI-crawler management shipped July 1 for every customer tier including free and marked the rollout complete Friday. The change reshapes how AI crawler traffic is authorised. Prior policy treated any AI bot as a single category. New policy sorts crawlers into three classes and lets sites set independent policies for each. Search covers crawlers that index content so a downstream model can answer questions about it later, with the expectation of referral traffic or another equitable compensation flow back to the site. Agent covers real-time automated activity on behalf of a person, including chat-fetch bots and browser-use agents. Training covers crawlers that pull content into pretraining or fine-tuning corpora.

The mechanical detail that matters is the multi-purpose crawler rule. Bots like Googlebot, Applebot and Bingbot serve both Search and Training use cases, and Cloudflare will apply the more restrictive of the two policies. A site that blocks Training but allows Search will end up blocking Googlebot in full unless Google splits its crawler user-agent, which it has publicly refused to do. Search Engine Journal flagged this on Friday as the single largest operational consequence of the change, and Cloudflare has not backed off the rule. Site owners face a real trade-off between preserving search-referral traffic and blocking training-corpus ingestion.

"On September 15, 2026, Cloudflare will set new defaults. For all new domains, Training and Agent will be blocked by default on pages that display ads. Search will remain allowed by default."— Cloudflare, AI traffic options changelog, July 1 2026

The September 15 default flip is the harder policy fact. Every new domain onboarded to Cloudflare from that date will block Agent and Training crawlers on ad-supported pages by default. Existing customers keep their current settings and can migrate at their own pace. Publishers who have been asking OpenAI, Anthropic and Google for content licensing deals now have a technical enforcement mechanism to bring to the negotiation table. Cloudflare has also opened the door to charging Agent and Training bots directly through a metered-access mechanism it has been prototyping in beta since April.

Robbyant, Ant Group's embodied-AI subsidiary that split out of Alibaba's Damo Academy earlier this year, open-sourced LingBot-Video and its full training report Thursday. LingBot-Video is a Mixture-of-Experts diffusion transformer trained end-to-end from scratch for video generation on embodied-intelligence workloads. The corpus is the interesting piece. Robbyant blends public web video with 70,000-plus hours of embodied recordings, which include first-person robot manipulation footage, teleoperation logs from Robbyant's LingBot-World deployments, and simulated rollouts from the Isaac Sim benchmark suite. Two architectural details matter. Second, Robbyant ships a specialised data augmentation pipeline and a multi-dimensional reward system tuned specifically for embodied-intelligence criteria: physical plausibility, temporal consistency across multi-step trajectories, and manipulation-relevant contact modelling. The result is a checkpoint that ranks top on the RBench Leaderboard as of Robbyant's release day, ahead of the Google DeepMind Genie 3 and Meta VJEPA-2 open baselines.

"LingBot-Video is the first open-source large-scale MoE video generation model dedicated to embodied intelligence."— Robbyant technical report abstract, July 9 2026
