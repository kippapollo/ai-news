Cadence ships AuraStack, the first agentic AI platform for PCB and advanced chip packaging

Cadence Design Systems opened AuraStack for orders on Wednesday, framing it as the industry's first agentic AI platform for printed circuit boards and advanced chip packaging. The platform runs on Nvidia hardware, taps TSMC as the co-development partner for advanced packaging convergence, and lets customers plug in Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini or open-source weights as the underlying model. Cadence claims up to a 50 percent cut in time-to-market and up to a 15-fold productivity lift on individual tasks, with the rollout completing in September.

Forbes · 2026-07-15 · Source ↗

Cadence Design Systems introduced AuraStack AI Super Agent on Wednesday, calling it the world's first agentic AI platform for printed circuit board and advanced chip packaging design. Engineers describe goals in plain English. AuraStack plans a workflow, calls the existing Cadence Allegro X and Integrity 3D-IC tools underneath, lays out the routing, virtually tests the design, and hands back a completed file. Cadence CEO Anirudh Devgan pitched it Wednesday as the delegation model coming to hardware: the human sets intent, the agent runs the physical layout task with the same tools an EDA engineer uses today.

The productivity claims are concrete. Cadence says AuraStack cuts overall time-to-market by up to 50 percent and drives up to 15-fold productivity gains on individual tasks such as trace routing, placement, and design-rule verification. Availability lands in stages this year, with the full rollout completing in September. Pricing was not disclosed. The platform runs on Nvidia GPUs for the compute layer, with Nvidia GB200 and CUDA-X libraries pre-integrated, and Cadence and TSMC signed a co-development partnership to accelerate advanced-package implementation through AI-driven automation, framed as helping customers hit "timely design convergence for increasingly complex multi-die systems."

"AuraStack is the world's first agentic AI platform for PCB and advanced packaging. It allows engineers to focus on high-value creative work while the agent handles the mechanical convergence steps."— Anirudh Devgan, Cadence CEO

Model-agnostic by design

The most striking product decision is that AuraStack does not lock customers to one model. Cadence customers can pair the agent with OpenAI's ChatGPT, Anthropic's Claude, Google's Gemini or open-source models, then swap between them per task. Devgan told analysts the design intent, the tool integrations, and the physical simulation harness are all Cadence's; the language model is the interchangeable head. For enterprise EDA customers who have compliance reasons to run one model in one workflow and another elsewhere, that flexibility matters more than raw benchmark scores.

"Customers can pair AuraStack with the AI model of their choice — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or open-source weights — because the physical design layer is what we own."— Cadence press release, July 15

Early customers and the Nvidia stack

Cadence confirmed Nvidia, TSMC, and Schneider Electric as early adopters. Nvidia's role is both customer and technology partner: AuraStack runs on Nvidia GB200 systems and taps CUDA-X libraries for the routing and simulation kernels. Schneider Electric is deploying AuraStack for its industrial-controls board designs. TSMC is using it inside its advanced-packaging convergence flows for HBM4 and CoWoS-L 2.5D stacks. The Register described the announcement as "AuraStack melds AI with HPC to speed PCB and advanced packaging design," pointing to the tight coupling between Cadence's simulator, Nvidia's compute, and the LLM planning layer.

Why this lands now

Every major chip designer is hunting for productivity gains as advanced-node designs get harder. TSMC's advanced packaging capacity is sold out through 2027. Nvidia holds most of it. Design cycles for HBM4 chiplet stacks and CoWoS-L are the bottleneck at the front end of the pipeline, and Cadence's argument is that AI agents can compress those cycles by half. The bear case is straightforward: virtual engineers still fail on the hard physical corners, and PCB design is one of those disciplines where a single missed impedance target means a respin. Cadence's counter is that AuraStack uses the same simulator and design-rule engines EDA engineers already trust; the agent orchestrates, it does not replace the ground-truth tool.

Estevez confirms Nvidia H200 shipments into China have begun, three years after the initial block

Voice of Emirates · 2026-07-15 · Source ↗

US Under Secretary for Industry and Security Alan Estevez told the House Foreign Affairs Committee Wednesday that first H200 units have arrived in China under the January 13 rule, ending a three-year block. Alibaba, Tencent and ByteDance sit among the ten approved buyers.

US Under Secretary of Commerce for Industry and Security Alan Estevez confirmed Wednesday in testimony before the House Foreign Affairs Committee that first shipments of Nvidia H200 accelerators have arrived in China. The confirmation ends a three-year policy stand-off dating to the October 2022 Biden-era controls and their October 2023 tightening, which had blocked the H200's export path into the country entirely. The reversal traces to President Trump's December 2025 announcement that Nvidia would be permitted to sell H200 chips to China, codified into a formal Bureau of Industry and Security rule on January 13, 2026.

The commercial stakes are large. The H200 is roughly six times more powerful than the H20, the compliance chip Nvidia designed for the previous export-control regime. Tom's Hardware reported earlier this month that Nvidia is preparing shipments of up to 82,000 GPUs, subject to a 25 percent tax under the licensing arrangement. The Department of Commerce approved requests in May from about ten major Chinese firms, including Alibaba, Tencent and ByteDance, to acquire H200 accelerators. Actual deliveries had not commenced by July 1. Estevez's Wednesday statement is the first official confirmation from a sitting Commerce official that shipments have moved.

"Shipments of Nvidia H200 chips have already begun arriving in China."— Alan Estevez, US Under Secretary for Industry and Security

What still stands and what the reversal buys

The advanced-node chips remain restricted. B100, B200, GB200 and Blackwell Ultra products cannot be shipped to China under current rules. The H200 sits one notch below those in Nvidia's product ladder but comfortably above the H20 the H200 replaces on the export list. Analysts frame the reversal as a bet that keeping Nvidia's ecosystem, CUDA and NVLink dominant inside Chinese data centers is worth ceding some raw performance ground, versus the alternative in which Chinese hyperscalers accelerate their pivot to Huawei Ascend and homegrown accelerators. The Brookings piece last month captured the counterargument bluntly: with restrictions on the top of the stack still in place, the H200 flow slows but does not stop Chinese sovereignty pushes on inference silicon.

For Nvidia's revenue mix, the reopening is material. China accounted for roughly 25 percent of data-center revenue at peak. The 25 percent tax pockets some of the upside for the US Treasury, but the shift widens Nvidia's addressable market at a moment when the company's global backlog was already exceeding capacity. On Wednesday's headlines, Nvidia shares rose in New York trading. The company has not commented publicly on shipment volumes.

Microsoft ships record 570-CVE July Patch Tuesday, credits AI-powered vulnerability discovery for the surge

gHacks · 2026-07-15 · Source ↗

The largest single Patch Tuesday drop on record fixes 59 critical bugs, three zero-days including an ADFS privilege bug and a SharePoint RCE already under active exploit, and a Copilot RCE (CVE-2026-48561). Microsoft attributes the flaw count to an internal AI-powered vulnerability discovery system running across the Windows codebase.

Microsoft shipped the July 2026 Patch Tuesday drop Tuesday, addressing 570 vulnerabilities across the Windows and Microsoft product stack. That is the largest count Microsoft has ever landed in a single monthly release. The prior high sat under 300. The breakdown lands 254 elevation-of-privilege bugs, 145 remote-code-execution bugs, 102 information-disclosure bugs, 35 denial-of-service, 17 security-feature-bypass, and 16 spoofing bugs. Fifty-nine of the 570 are rated Critical.

Three zero-days are on the list. CVE-2026-56155 in Active Directory Federation Services allows attackers to gain administrative privileges through insufficient access-control granularity and is already under active exploitation. CVE-2026-56164 in Microsoft SharePoint Server permits remote privilege escalation via missing authentication on a critical function, also being exploited in the wild. CVE-2026-50661 is a publicly-disclosed BitLocker bypass that permits an attacker with brief physical access to reach encrypted data. Windows 11 users get the fixes via KB5101650 and KB5099414; Windows 10 Extended Security Updates arrive via KB5099539.

"A new AI-powered vulnerability discovery system that identifies security flaws across the Windows codebase before attackers can exploit them."— Microsoft, on the record patch count

Copilot enters the CVE list

CVE-2026-48561 patches a critical-severity remote-code-execution bug in Microsoft Copilot. Full technical details are held back per Microsoft's coordinated disclosure practice. The item continues 2026's running theme of Copilot-branded features appearing in the Patch Tuesday releases as attackers probe the LLM-integration surface for prompt-injection paths that reach real code execution. gHacks noted the RCE marked the first time an AI assistant CVE has cleared the critical bar in a Patch Tuesday release since the classification was added last year.

Why the count spiked

Microsoft explicitly tied the surge to internal automation. The company said an AI-powered vulnerability discovery system now runs continuously against the Windows codebase, surfacing flaws faster than the manual security research pipeline previously could. Whether attackers have equivalent tooling is now the operational question. Independent researchers have noted for months that automated LLM-driven vulnerability discovery is inside the arsenal of both defenders and offensive actors; Microsoft's decision to disclose that it uses one at the source-tree level is the first public admission from a major vendor. The knock-on question is patching cadence. IT teams that manage large Windows fleets already struggled with the 200-plus CVE months. A record 570 in one drop lands on the same operators, and Microsoft has not announced pipeline changes to help enterprise deployment ride the new pace.

"Jetson Thor is the ultimate platform for physical AI. The new T3000 and T2000 modules bring Blackwell compute into a form factor that lets partners ship at scale."— Nvidia, official product blog

The physical-AI compute market is Nvidia's stated growth axis for 2027, and Jetson Thor is the enabling silicon. Whether the T2000 finds a customer base against Qualcomm's Snapdragon Ride and the growing catalog of RISC-V edge accelerators is the open competitive question. On raw teraflops, T2000 leads. Anthropic tags Claude Code v2.1.210 with a live elapsed-time counter, worktree isolation fix, and Agent-tool prompt-injection hardening

Releasebot · 2026-07-15 · Source ↗

Long-running tool calls now visibly tick in the collapsed summary line so they no longer look stuck. A worktree-isolation bug where subagents ran git commands against the main repo is fixed. The Agent tool has new defenses against indirect prompt injection.

Anthropic tagged Claude Code v2.1.210 Wednesday. The user-facing feature is a live elapsed-time counter attached to the collapsed tool-call summary line, giving long-running tool calls a visible tick so operators no longer have to guess whether the agent is working or wedged. The change addresses one of the top-voted complaints in the Claude Code GitHub tracker over the past month. For agent operators running multi-hour builds or benchmark suites through the CLI, the counter also functions as a rough progress signal.

The bigger changes sit beneath the UI. Anthropic fixed a class of bugs where isolation: 'worktree' subagents were executing git commands against the main repository rather than their isolated worktrees. That was a real correctness issue for the growing set of teams using the worktree pattern to run multiple agents on the same codebase in parallel: the isolation guarantee was silently broken for git operations, meaning a git-commit call inside subagent A could land against the main worktree. The v2.1.210 fix returns the isolation to its documented behavior.

"Hardened the Agent tool against indirect prompt injection vulnerabilities."— Claude Code v2.1.210 release notes, July 15

Indirect prompt injection

The security highlight is the release note "Hardened the Agent tool against indirect prompt injection vulnerabilities." Anthropic did not publish exploit details. The class of bug involves an attacker inserting malicious instructions into content the agent reads (a document, a webpage, a file the agent Grep's) that the LLM then treats as commands. Claude Code's Agent tool sits at a high-privilege position in the tool ecosystem: it can spawn subagents, call other tools, and write to disk. Hardening the surface against injection paths is baseline hygiene, and Anthropic's willingness to ship the fix in a point release rather than gate it to a major indicates the team is treating agent-tool injection as an active area rather than a latent risk.

The full list

Other fixes in v2.1.210: resolved "job not found" and "agent is still starting" errors during session transitions; fixed background workers, MCP servers, and approval mechanisms for stronger stability; corrected hook callback timeouts previously being misreported as user rejections; screen-reader mode now announces permission mode changes audibly; Agent view improvements with persistent selection marking; Grep pagination bug fixed; Windows plugin cache failures resolved; background-worker crash-loop scenarios patched; late-appearing symlinks now reconciled into sandbox deny-write lists. Anthropic also added startup warnings for deprecated permission-rule syntax, nudging users toward Edit(path) and Read(path) forms. The upgrade path is a standard npm bump; no manifest changes required for existing agent scripts.

b10016 ships SYCL Flash Attention with XMX engine via oneDNN. b10032 adds a CUDA lightning-indexer wmma kernel. b10021 is a DeepSeek V4 graph-split reduction. b10034 excludes MoE kernels on Adreno A7x to work around compiler miscompilation.

ggml-org tagged ten llama.cpp builds through the UTC day on July 15, landing between 09:10 UTC (b10016) and 20:34 UTC (b10034). The headline is b10016: SYCL Flash Attention with the XMX engine via oneDNN, delivering a claimed 1.21× prefill speedup at certain sequence lengths on compatible Intel Battlemage hardware. The XMX engine is Intel's matrix-multiplication acceleration path on Arc B-series and Battlemage discrete GPUs. Wiring Flash Attention to it via oneDNN is the SYCL backend's equivalent of the CUDA cuBLAS Flash Attention integration on Nvidia parts.

The rest of the day fills the surrounding SYCL and CUDA gaps. b10017 raised the minimum USM system-allocation buffer threshold from 1 GiB to 4 GiB to enable more aggressive VRAM overcommit. b10018 added a SYCL xielu activation-function operator. b10019 implemented SYCL fp16 kernel type support for conv2d_dw operations. b10020 fixed SYCL get_rows for Q2_K, Q4_K and Q5_K quantization formats. b10025 relaxed CUDA tensor-contiguity requirements for quantized concatenation operations and added test cases for non-contiguous scenarios.

"opencl: exclude some moe kernels on Adreno a7x — addresses compiler miscompilation issues affecting MoE model weight handling on certain A7x devices."— llama.cpp b10034 release note

DeepSeek V4 and lightning-indexer

b10021 lands a DeepSeek V4 graph-split reduction optimization. DeepSeek V4 shipped with a 1.6-trillion-parameter Pro variant and 284-billion-parameter Flash variant in late June with a fine-grained sparse-attention architecture that stresses the KV-cache and graph-split heuristics in llama.cpp's CUDA backend. b10021's reduction targets the memory-transfer overhead in that architecture's forward pass. b10032 adds a CUDA implementation of the GGML_OP_LIGHTNING_INDEXER operation, providing both generic vector and wmma kernel variants and relaxing the architecture requirement to Turing compatibility. Lightning indexer is the primitive DeepSeek V4 uses for sparse attention lookups; landing it on CUDA broadens the hardware base for full V4 inference.

Housekeeping

b10031 dropped the --stdin mutual-exclusion check from the tokenize tool to align CLI behavior with completion scripts. b10034 excluded some MoE kernels on Adreno A7x mobile GPUs to work around a Qualcomm compiler miscompilation issue that produced silently wrong outputs on MoE model weight handling. The Adreno path is llama.cpp's route into Android inference on Snapdragon 8 Elite silicon, so the workaround protects a growing user base until Qualcomm's compiler fix lands. Georgi Gerganov has flagged July 21 as the target for the Hexagon NPU code drop that closes out the Snapdragon inference story.

Huazhong University of Science and Technology's VLRLab-OCR released MonkeyOCRv2 Wednesday, a visual-text foundation model for document AI that unifies text detection, layout parsing, table structure recognition, formula recognition, and multi-page reading-order inference in a single model. The v2 update sits atop the original MonkeyOCR released in early 2025 and roughly doubles document-understanding benchmark scores on DocVQA and OmniDocBench. Weights are permissively licensed on Hugging Face.

The unification story is what mattered to the community. Existing OSS document AI pipelines chain together separate models for each stage: PaddleOCR for text detection, LayoutLMv3 or PP-StructureV2 for structure, and Nougat or MathPix for equation extraction. Every hand-off is a place for errors to compound. MonkeyOCRv2 runs the entire pipeline through one visual-language transformer, and VLRLab-OCR reports substantial accuracy improvements on complex scientific papers precisely because the model treats formulas, tables, and body text as a single joint decoding problem rather than a sequence of independent tasks.

"MonkeyOCRv2 handles detection, layout, tables, and formulas in one pass. No pipeline glue."— VLRLab-OCR, MonkeyOCRv2 release

Where it lands
