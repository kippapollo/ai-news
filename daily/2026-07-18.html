Google DeepMind ships Gemini 3.5 Pro after a full rebuild, at $1.25 in and $10 out per million tokens

Gemini 3.5 Pro lands Friday six weeks past its June target with a 2-million-token context window, a Deep Think reasoning layer gated behind the $250-per-month Ultra tier, and a base API price that undercuts Claude Fable 5 by roughly 4x on output. Google scrapped the Gemini 2.5 architecture in late June after a coding-data retrain failed to close the gap with Anthropic and OpenAI. Timing collides with Xi Jinping's in-person Shanghai keynote and Kimi K3 shipping open weights a week earlier.

CentralJersey.com · 2026-07-17 · Source ↗

Google DeepMind released Gemini 3.5 Pro on Friday, July 17, ending a six-week slip past the June commitment made at Google I/O and closing a public-relations gap that had cost Alphabet shares 4.4 percent on Thursday following Bloomberg's account of a failed late-June coding-data retrain. The model that shipped is a full architectural rebuild, not an incremental step on the Gemini 2.5 Pro backbone. Google engineers, according to independent reporting corroborated by DeepMind's launch materials, threw out the 2.5 Pro architecture after finding structural failures in recursive tool-calling, scalable vector graphics scene generation, and image-quality regressions that a data patch could not repair.

The headline numbers on release: a 2-million-token context window, a new Deep Think reasoning layer gated to the $250-per-month Gemini Advanced Ultra tier, and API pricing at $1.25 per million input tokens and $10 per million output. That output rate lands roughly 4x under Claude Fable 5 and just above Meta's Muse Spark 1.1, positioning Gemini 3.5 Pro as a price-aggressive flagship for enterprise workloads that had drifted to Anthropic and OpenAI over the past four months. A refreshed Gemini 3.5 Flash ships alongside it, with an explicit repositioning after Bloomberg's Thursday piece flagged that the previous Flash generation was "more expensive than previous versions yet slower with reduced capabilities."

"We're shipping quickly across a wide range of models while keeping them highly cost-effective for customers."— Google spokesperson, cited by Bloomberg on Thursday

The Deep Think reasoning layer

Deep Think is a Gemini-native long-horizon reasoning surface, exposed only through the Ultra tier and the paid API, designed to break complex tasks into subtasks and execute them autonomously with minimal user intervention. On Google's own internal benchmarks the layer clears a wide margin over 2.5 Pro on multi-step tool use, structured proofs, and long-context synthesis over the 2-million-token window. The company is framing Deep Think as the direct competitive answer to OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol Ultra subagent mode announced July 9 and to Anthropic's Extended Thinking budget that shipped with Sonnet 5 and Claude Fable 5. What the third-party benchmark community measures over the next 72 hours will decide whether the Deep Think framing lands or reads as marketing.

Autonomous workflows and the enterprise pitch

Google's enterprise pitch centers on autonomous workflows. Rather than requiring explicit instructions per step, Gemini 3.5 Pro is presented as able to plan a multi-step task, execute against connected apps, and hand back a completed artifact. The framing tracks the direction OpenAI took with ChatGPT Work Friday and Meta took with Meta Business Agent this month. The pricing move is aggressive: at $1.25 input and $10 output per million tokens, Gemini 3.5 Pro undercuts Claude Fable 5 by roughly 4x on output and matches Meta's Muse Spark 1.1 tier while offering a nominally larger context window. Anthropic's mid-July price ladder came under coordinated pressure this week from Kimi K3, Muse Spark, and now Gemini 3.5 Pro.

"A ground-up redesign, engineered to close critical gaps in mathematical reasoning, scalable vector graphics scene generation, and overall image quality."— Google DeepMind launch framing, per BigGo Finance and TechTimes

The timing collision and the caveats

Gemini 3.5 Pro's Friday release lands on the same UTC day as Xi Jinping's Shanghai World AI Conference keynote, Huawei's Atlas 950 SuperPoD debut, TSMC's earnings and Arizona expansion, and Anthropic's Claude Code point release. The collision is not accidental. Google's internal debate over whether to accept an August ship date and cede a full month to Kimi K3 was resolved, according to Bloomberg's Thursday reporting, in favor of the Friday launch. The caveats are real. Every claim about specifications, pricing, and capabilities in third-party reporting through Thursday came from leaks, not official Google announcements. The June 2026 Gemini 3.5 Pro launch commitment came and went. Some early enterprise customers who moved to Claude Sonnet and GPT-5.6 Sol during the delay window will not migrate back on price alone. The next 30 days will measure whether the rebuild landed a real capability step or a repositioned narrative.

The wider read across the Shanghai and Silicon Valley calendars: three model families now sit inside touching distance on frontier coding evaluations, and each of the four US labs is now pricing at or below the DeepSeek-derived open-weight floor. What was a differentiated-capability market in Q1 2026 is now a differentiated-workflow market. That reprices the value of vertical integration for Google (Search, Workspace, Android), Microsoft (Copilot, Azure, GitHub), and Anthropic (Claude Code, MCP). Meta and xAI are pricing the same play a different way. Gemini 3.5 Pro is the Google entry.

Anthropic ships Claude Code stability release with 200-subagent-per-session cap and hardened permission previews

Anthropic release notes · 2026-07-17 · Source ↗

Adds --forward-subagent-text and CLAUDE_CODE_FORWARD_SUBAGENT_TEXT for stream-json transparency, defaults to Claude Opus 4.8 on Bedrock and Vertex, and neutralizes bidirectional-override, zero-width, and lookalike quote characters in permission previews to block visual approval spoofing.

Anthropic tagged a Claude Code stability and quality update on Friday, July 17, that lands as the most substantive Claude Code point release of the month. The headline changes: a new --forward-subagent-text flag and matching CLAUDE_CODE_FORWARD_SUBAGENT_TEXT environment variable that expose subagent thinking-and-response text into stream-json output for observability tools; a per-session hard cap on subagent spawns defaulting to 200 with CLAUDE_CODE_MAX_SUBAGENTS_PER_SESSION as the override; a default model swap to Claude Opus 4.8 on Amazon Bedrock, Google Vertex AI, and the AWS Marketplace listing; and a security hardening pass on permission previews so that tool-input strings cannot visually overwrite the approval prompt shown to the user.

"Permission previews relayed to chat channels were fixed to neutralize bidirectional-override, zero-width, and lookalike quote characters, so tool inputs cannot visually alter the approval message."— Anthropic Claude Code changelog, July 17

The subagent cap and the runaway-delegation problem

The 200-subagent-per-session cap is a direct fix for a known failure mode where a background Claude Code session recursively delegates work to fresh subagents faster than the operator can spot. The bug had surfaced in high-fanout research workflows and in agent-driven test runs over the June to July window. Setting a hard default cap moves the guardrail from soft-warning to hard-fail, which is consistent with the direction Anthropic has been running Claude Code since the v2.1 series. The related fix for returning to claude agents silently stopping running subagents and re-running prompts from scratch means work-in-progress carries over across session switches.

The prompt-injection hardening

The permission-preview hardening is the second-order response to the OpenAI GPT-Red disclosure Thursday. The vulnerability class Anthropic patched: an attacker crafts tool input that includes Unicode bidirectional-override characters, zero-width joiners, and lookalike quotation marks that render as "cd /tmp" in the approval prompt but execute as "cd /etc" once approved. The hardening pass canonicalizes the string, strips bidi overrides, and normalizes lookalike quotes so the operator sees the exact bytes that will run. Anthropic's Trust and Safety team has documented the specific attack payloads that motivated the fix.

"A new --forward-subagent-text flag and CLAUDE_CODE_FORWARD_SUBAGENT_TEXT environment variable were added to include subagent text and thinking in stream-json output."— Claude Code changelog, July 17

The default-model swap

Swapping the default model to Claude Opus 4.8 on Bedrock, Vertex and AWS Marketplace is a routine version-bump, but it comes with pricing implications. Enterprise customers running Claude Code against Bedrock had been defaulting to Sonnet 5 for cost reasons; the Opus 4.8 default now costs roughly 5x on output tokens. Customers who want to keep Sonnet as default need to set the CLAUDE_CODE_MODEL environment variable explicitly. Anthropic's pricing story remains under pressure from Kimi K3 at $3/$15 for a 2.8T MoE and Gemini 3.5 Pro at $1.25/$10 for a rebuilt frontier model. The July 17 Claude Code update makes clear that the Anthropic play is depth of integration into MCP, Bedrock, Vertex, and the Microsoft 365 connector rather than headline API-per-token pricing.

VideoChat3 tops Hugging Face daily papers, 4-billion-parameter fully open video MLLM covers motion, hour-long stories, and live streams

Hugging Face · 2026-07-17 · Source ↗

Nanjing University's Multimedia Computing Group ships a fully open 4B video multimodal model built on an Inflated 3D Vision Transformer with adaptive frame-resolution sampling. Second on the daily list is SEED for self-evolving on-policy distillation, third is SearchOS-V1 for open-domain search agents.

VideoChat3 landed at the top of the Hugging Face daily papers surface on Friday, July 17, as a fully open 4-billion-parameter video multimodal large language model from Nanjing University's Multimedia Computing Group. The model is positioned as a generalist video-understanding backbone that handles subtle motion, hour-long narrative reasoning, precise temporal grounding, and live-stream understanding, all at inference-time cost that is competitive with 7B image-only baselines. The GitHub repository at MCG-NJU/VideoChat3 launched with weights, code, evaluation, and an inference server, under a permissive open license.

The architecture bet

Two design choices carry the paper. First, an Inflated 3D Vision Transformer, I3D-ViT, that lifts a pretrained 2D image vision transformer into a spatiotemporal backbone by inflating the tokenizer without a full retrain. Second, an Adaptive Frame Resolution for Streaming Video Perception layer that dynamically adjusts the frame-sampling rate and per-frame resolution based on scene complexity, letting the model process a two-hour movie and a five-second GIF with proportional compute. The efficiency claim: 4B active parameters can reach parity with 13B-parameter video baselines at a fraction of the inference cost, which if it holds outside the paper's own evaluation moves video understanding into the reachable-on-consumer-GPU range.

"VideoChat3 is a fully open, efficient, and generalist video-centric MLLM that advances video understanding through two complementary designs."— VideoChat3 paper abstract, MCG-NJU

The rest of the daily surface

Second on the Hugging Face daily papers list Friday was SEED, "Self-Evolving On-Policy Distillation for Agentic Reinforcement Learning," a method for turning teacher-model traces into student policies without a fresh RL run. Third was SearchOS-V1, positioning a robust open-domain information-seeking agent architecture with heterogeneous tool routing. The Friday surface reflects the July research direction Simon Willison flagged earlier in the week: distillation-driven agent training, retrieval-augmented reasoning, and open video understanding all landing in the same 24-hour window.

"A 4B generalist video MLLM built to understand video across time, from subtle motion and hour-long stories to precise temporal evidence and live streams."— MCG-NJU project description

Why it matters

Open video-language models had been stuck at 7B-to-13B scale on architectures inherited from LLaVA, with real-time video understanding running slow enough to preclude live-stream applications. VideoChat3's I3D-ViT plus Adaptive Frame Resolution combination pushes the compute envelope down without dropping the capability floor, and the fully-open release model, including the training recipe, means labs building on top can retrain against domain-specific video corpora without renegotiating a license. For robotics teams, video-agent developers, and long-form content researchers, VideoChat3 is likely the July baseline moving forward, replacing InternVideo2 and LLaVA-Video across many published benchmarks over the next 60 days.
