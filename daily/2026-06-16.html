Seventy-six cybersecurity professionals tell the White House to lift the Anthropic ban

Alex Stamos, Katie Moussouris, Jon Callas, Paul Vixie, Dino Dai Zovi, Casey Ellis and Rachel Tobac signed an open letter Monday calling the worldwide suspension of Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 "dangerous" and saying the underlying jailbreak Amazon researchers demonstrated to the administration on June 11 "cannot meaningfully be fixed." Anthropic technical staff are inside the Commerce Department in Washington for in-person meetings. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth defended the three-month-old Pentagon blacklist on X. Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney told reporters from Ireland the episode shows the risk of overreliance on a small set of US frontier-model providers and applied the prudential-regulation term "model risk" to AI for the first time at a G7-leader level. Cohere CEO Aidan Gomez told Bloomberg his company is seeing "huge inbound" and is tripling its London office. The administration has not signaled a timeline for restoration.

TechCrunch · CNBC · Bloomberg · 2026-06-15 · Source ↗ ·

The Anthropic export-control crisis entered its fourth day Monday with the first organised public response from outside the company. Seventy-six cybersecurity practitioners including Alex Stamos, Casey Ellis, Jon Callas, Paul Vixie, Dino Dai Zovi, Katie Moussouris and Rachel Tobac signed an open letter calling the Commerce Department directive that disabled Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 globally "dangerous" and asking the administration to lift it. Their argument is narrow and technical, not political. The Amazon research paper that triggered the Friday June 12 export-control order describes a workaround in which Fable 5 reads code and identifies flaws, the letter says — capability that the cybersecurity industry uses to defend its own systems and that competitors including DeepSeek, Kimi K2.7 Code and the open-weights tier can already perform. Pulling that capability from defenders while open-weights labs catch up, the signatories argue, leaves attackers structurally ahead.

"To pull the best capabilities away from defenders without a good reason when our adversaries are rapidly advancing is dangerous."— Open letter to the US government, 2026-06-15

Inside the Commerce Department, day four

Anthropic senior technical staff are inside the US Commerce Department in Washington on Monday for in-person meetings with administration officials, CNBC reported. The company has been in daily contact with the administration since the Friday directive landed at 5:21pm ET. CEO Dario Amodei is scheduled to sit beside Sam Altman and Demis Hassabis at the G7 working lunch on AI in Évian-les-Bains on Wednesday June 17, which gives the meetings a 48-hour resolution window. Bloomberg framed the directive as a US policy "reversal" against an earlier executive order pledging no AI licensing regime, citing former Trump AI czar David Sacks's claim on X over the weekend that Anthropic refused to remediate before controls landed and Anthropic's counter that the cited jailbreak was "narrow rather than a full jailbreak" and not unique to its models. The two accounts remain irreconcilable on the public record. Allspring Global Investments portfolio manager Gary Tan told CNBC that US frontier models are now "increasingly treated as strategic assets, with access tightly controlled and shaped by national-security considerations" — language that did not exist in the trade literature a month ago.

"The behavior described in the paper cannot meaningfully be fixed, and any attempt would only weaken the model for defense."— Katie Moussouris, founder, Luta Security

The Pentagon doubles down

Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth used Monday to defend the Pentagon's three-month-old supply-chain-risk designation against Anthropic. "Three months ago, the Department of War kicked Anthropic AI out of our building forever. Each passing day shows that this was the right move," Hegseth posted on X. The blacklist was issued after Anthropic refused to allow its models to be used for autonomous lethal weapons or mass domestic surveillance. Anthropic CFO Krishna Rao said before the Commerce action that the Pentagon ban alone could cost the company "multiple billions" in 2026 revenue. The TechCrunch analysis on June 15 sharpened the read further: the Fable 5 order is not really a jailbreak story, it argued, but the next escalation in a months-long political fight between the administration and Anthropic over autonomous-weapons and surveillance use cases. The cybersecurity letter implicitly endorses that reading by saying the Amazon paper's technical claim "cannot meaningfully be fixed" — implying that the safety rationale offered for the suspension is unfalsifiable and therefore cannot be the real reason.

Carney's "model risk" and Cohere's "huge inbound"

Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney, a former Bank of England and Bank of Canada governor, told reporters in Westport, Ireland that "the situation we're in collectively right now with Mythos and Fable is something that can happen with overreliance on certain models" and that "it is never a good idea to have one option." His use of the precise prudential-regulation phrase "model risk" — the term financial regulators use for institutional dependence on a single quantitative model — was the first time a sitting G7 leader applied that vocabulary to AI in public. European Commission spokesperson Thomas Regnier reiterated through the weekend that any US contingency measures "should not be discriminatory against partners" and confirmed Brussels is assessing the practical fallout for EU users. Renew Europe MEPs Bart Groothuis and Christophe Grudler called the action a "wake-up call" and demanded accelerated EU LLM and open-weights investment, with Groothuis warning Europe risks "digital colonization." The market response was immediate. Cohere CEO Aidan Gomez told Bloomberg his Toronto-headquartered company is seeing "huge inbound" from non-US customers, announced he is tripling Cohere's London office, and called the Anthropic directive a "massive wake-up call" for global enterprise buyers. Mistral advanced talks for a €3 billion round at a €20 billion valuation, nearly double its €11.7 billion September 2025 mark. India's Sarvam AI closed first close of a $234 million Series B at $1.5 billion led by a $150 million HCLTech strategic check — the first major Indian AI funding round after the suspension hit the world's second-largest Claude market. Anthropic still has no public timeline for restoration.

Why "model risk" is the word that matters

Inside banking supervision, "model risk" is a regulated category. Institutions must inventory which quantitative models they depend on, prove they can switch them off without operational failure, and maintain alternatives. Carney's invocation of the term re-categorises foundation models from product input to systemic dependency. More than 70 percent of Canadian exports go to the United States, and Carney coupled the AI remark with the same diversification argument he is using on trade. Renew Europe MEPs Bart Groothuis and Christophe Grudler picked up the framing within hours, calling the suspension a "wake-up call" and saying Europe needs its own LLMs and open-weights models or risks "digital colonization." European Commission spokesperson Thomas Regnier added that any US contingency measures "should not be discriminatory against partners." With Carney, Groothuis, Grudler, Regnier and India's Mohandas Pai all using sovereignty language on the same weekend, the political coalition for non-US frontier-model capacity is now the largest it has been since the Brussels AI Act process. The Évian working lunch will be the first time the G7 leaders' table treats AI as financial-stability adjacent rather than as a Brussels-and-Beijing trade question.
