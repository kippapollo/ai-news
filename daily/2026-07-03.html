Anthropic opens Samsung 2nm chip talks, joining every hyperscaler with in-house AI silicon plans

The Information first, then Bloomberg and TechCrunch, confirm early-stage work on a custom Anthropic AI accelerator with Samsung Electronics as prospective fab and packaging partner on the SF2 node. The company recently hired Clive Chan, an early member of OpenAI's chip team. Google, Amazon, Meta and Microsoft all already run in-house silicon; OpenAI unveiled the Broadcom-designed Jalapeño inference chip last month.

The Information · Bloomberg · TechCrunch · 2026-07-02 · Source ↗ ·

The Information broke it first on Thursday: Anthropic has begun early-stage design work on a custom AI accelerator and opened talks with Samsung Electronics about manufacturing it on Samsung Foundry's SF2 two-nanometer process, with Samsung's advanced packaging line as the second half of the ask. Bloomberg and TechCrunch confirmed the report within hours. The company is still deciding what the processor should do, how powerful it should be, and how it should sit inside a server, so nothing about the design is fixed. What is fixed is the intent: Anthropic wants to stop being the only frontier lab shipping product without its own silicon underneath.

Google runs Trillium and the TPU v7 for both training and inference. Amazon has Trainium 3 and Inferentia deployed at scale across AWS Bedrock. Meta ramped MTIA v3 through the first half of 2026 as the workhorse for ranking and open-model serving. Microsoft's MAIA 200 is inside Copilot production regions. OpenAI unveiled its first Broadcom-designed inference chip, codenamed Jalapeño, in June. Every frontier hyperscaler except Anthropic was already on its own silicon roadmap. Thursday's report closes that gap on paper.

Anthropic has begun early-stage work on a custom AI chip and held talks with Samsung Electronics as a potential manufacturing partner.— The Information, 2026-07-02

The Samsung angle matters more than the chip

Samsung Foundry has been fighting to keep leading-edge customers since TSMC pulled ahead on N2. SF2 is Samsung's second-generation gate-all-around node and the process the company is betting its foundry business on. Anthropic joining Samsung on SF2 would put the process alongside a customer with defensible frontier-model workloads, which is the piece Samsung's pitch to Qualcomm, Nvidia and OpenAI has been missing. The Information notes Anthropic is also considering Samsung's advanced packaging facilities. SAINT, Samsung's 3D stacking family, is the plausible target, aligning with how OpenAI's Jalapeño uses Broadcom-designed compute stacked on HBM4.

The Samsung Electronics stock is up on the report in Seoul. Analysts on the report attribute the move to the strategic signal, not the immediate dollar value: the design contract itself may end up small, but Samsung being publicly named as Anthropic's foundry partner reads across the whole SF2 pipeline. Amazon's continuing $8 billion equity commitment in Anthropic already routes the frontier lab's training workloads through Trainium, and the Google Cloud deal that closed in May pipes additional capacity through TPU v7. Anthropic's public compute story has been that it is the only frontier lab with contracted access to all three major accelerator families, an argument the Amazon-Google-Nvidia access story was built around. A custom Anthropic chip is the argument that the lab has now graduated from renting silicon strategies to owning one.

Anthropic's plans are at an early stage and the company is still determining what the processor should do, how powerful it should be and how it would fit into a server.— The Information, paraphrased by TechCrunch, 2026-07-02

Clive Chan and the hiring signal

Anthropic recently brought on Clive Chan, an early member of OpenAI's own custom-chip team. Chan's OpenAI work fed into the Jalapeño design cycle with Broadcom, so the hire imports both the technical and organisational muscle Anthropic did not previously have. Bloomberg reports the company has been hiring silicon engineers, packaging specialists and RTL leads through the second quarter, though headcount inside the effort remains small. A serious frontier accelerator programme typically runs three to five years from first design freeze to production silicon. Anthropic is still in the definition phase, so first tape-out would not be expected before 2028 at the earliest.

The project is still nascent, no detailed design or manufacturing work has begun, and the company may not proceed.— The Information, 2026-07-02

What this means for the "sovereign silicon" argument

Wednesday's Meituan LongCat 2.0 open-source release, which trained end to end on a 50,000-card domestic Chinese ASIC cluster, is the strategic backdrop against which Anthropic's Samsung talks read. Beijing has demonstrated a national-scale AI supply chain independent of Nvidia. Europe has been asking Brussels to "pursue the strategic establishment of Anthropic" inside the EU. The US frontier labs need to show they can operate on non-Nvidia silicon too, both to reduce cost-of-serve and to answer the sovereignty pressure explicitly. Samsung's SF2 line runs in South Korea, which places Anthropic's silicon supply chain outside the direct Taiwan-Strait exposure that TSMC's N2 introduces. The strategic geography of the deal is at least as important as its technical content.

The catch is that Anthropic may not proceed. All three sources emphasise the design is unspecified, the volume commitments are absent, and the deal is preliminary. The company is publicly leaving the door open to walking away. But even the fact that the news is public reshapes the pricing conversation with Nvidia, Google Cloud and AWS. A frontier-lab customer walking around with a plausible in-house silicon story on paper is a stronger negotiator on the next commitment cycle. That is the near-term operational effect of the Samsung report, whether or not tape-out ever happens.

Claude Code 2.1.198 ships Chrome GA, PR-opening background agents, /dataviz and agent notification hooks

Anthropic release notes · Releasebot · 2026-07-02 · Source ↗ ·

Background agents now commit, push and open draft pull requests when finishing code work. A new Notification hook fires on agent_needs_input and agent_completed. Claude in Chrome goes generally available. The /dataviz skill for chart and dashboard design ships in the same tag.

Claude Code 2.1.198 tagged Thursday, roughly 18 hours after Wednesday's 2.1.197 default-model flip to Sonnet 5. The release makes Claude in Chrome generally available across all supported platforms, closes several reliability issues, and adds three product features that materially change how background agents behave.

The first is the git integration. Background agents now commit, push and open draft pull requests when they finish a code-writing task, without a user in the loop. That closes the last piece of the autonomous-coding workflow. Previously the agent finished the diff and stopped; the reviewer had to open the PR. In 2.1.198 the reviewer gets the PR in their queue when the diff is ready.

The second is the Notification hook, with two events: agent_needs_input and agent_completed. Both fire out to a user-configured endpoint, letting a background agent pause work and page a human operator through Slack, email or a custom webhook when it hits an ambiguity or when it lands the final result. That is the piece that lets long-running background agents run overnight without a human process babysitting them.

Background agents now commit, push, and open draft PRs when finishing code work.— Anthropic, Claude Code 2.1.198 release notes, 2026-07-02
Claude in Chrome is generally available.— Anthropic, Claude Code 2.1.198 release notes, 2026-07-02

/dataviz and the skills expansion

The third feature is the /dataviz skill: a preconfigured Claude Code skill for producing charts, dashboards and stat-tile UIs on demand inside a session. It builds on the "dataviz" skill Anthropic published for the main Claude.ai product earlier in June and imports the same colour-system-aware defaults, sequential-and-diverging palette validation, and legend and axis mark specs. The skill is designed to intercept any request to render a table with 4-plus rows or 3-plus columns and route to a styled HTML output instead of ASCII. This closes a common failure mode where agents produce plain-text tables that read poorly and disable the reviewer's ability to interrogate the data.

The bundle also fixed a mid-response retry regression that had appeared in 2.1.196 when the network drop landed inside a streamed tool call, and cleaned up several agents-view refresh issues introduced by the Sonnet 5 default. The 2.1.198 tag is the operational baseline enterprise customers were waiting for before rolling Sonnet 5 to team-wide default.
