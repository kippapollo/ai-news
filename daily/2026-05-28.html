01

Anthropic closes $30B-plus round at a $900B valuation, overtaking OpenAI

Anthropic is finalizing a financing of more than $30 billion at a pre-money valuation above $900 billion, co-led by Sequoia, Dragoneer, Altimeter and Greenoaks, with each firm putting in roughly $2 billion. The round, which assembled in a matter of weeks after inbound interest in late April, would make Claude's maker the most valuable private AI company in the world, surpassing OpenAI's $852 billion mark set in March. Reports suggest an IPO could follow as early as October.

Cognition raises $1B at a $26B valuation as Devin's enterprise base balloons

ByteDance weighs up to $70B in capex as it races to scale AI infrastructure

ByteDance is internally discussing capital expenditure of as much as $70 billion this year to build out data centers and AI infrastructure, more than doubling its 2025 spend, according to Bloomberg. The company is debating how aggressively to fund the buildout against rising memory and accelerator costs, with much of the spend expected to come from its roughly $50 billion in 2025 profit. The figure caps a week of upward revisions across Chinese AI capex plans amid global compute scarcity.

China extends overseas-travel curbs on AI talent to Alibaba and DeepSeek

Beijing has expanded existing travel restrictions on government AI researchers to private-sector founders, researchers and executives at firms including Alibaba and DeepSeek, requiring agency approval before international trips. Officials are determining who falls under the restrictions based on strategic relevance rather than job title, signaling that Beijing increasingly treats private AI work as national-security infrastructure. The expansion follows similar restrictions on DeepSeek leaders quietly imposed in late 2025.

08

Gemini API ships a breaking change to its Interactions schema

Google flipped the new Interactions API schema on as the default in the Gemini API on May 26, with the legacy schema set to be removed entirely on June 8. Developers building on Gemini have a roughly two-week window to migrate request and response shapes or risk production breakage. The change lands on the heels of Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite's general availability and a sustained push from Google to standardize the API surface for high-volume agentic workloads.

09

Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite goes generally available at $0.25 / million input tokens

Google promoted Gemini 3.1 Flash-Lite to general availability across the Gemini API and Vertex AI, pricing it at $0.25 per million input tokens and $1.50 per million output tokens. Google says the model delivers 2.5× faster time-to-first-token and 45% higher output throughput versus 2.5 Flash, with multimodal support across text, image, video, audio and PDF inputs. Configurable "thinking" levels let developers trade reasoning depth against latency on the fly — a tooling choice clearly aimed at high-volume agentic workflows.

10

U.S. Commerce expands frontier-model pre-deployment testing to five labs

The Center for AI Standards and Innovation, housed in the U.S. Department of Commerce, now has voluntary pre-deployment access to frontier models from every major American AI developer after Google DeepMind, Microsoft and xAI joined OpenAI and Anthropic earlier this month. Testing runs through the TRAINS Taskforce, with NIH handling biosecurity, the national labs covering chemical and nuclear proliferation, and DoD and DHS leading cybersecurity work. The framework gives the federal government its widest structural visibility yet into unreleased frontier models.
