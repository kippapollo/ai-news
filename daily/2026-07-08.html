SpaceXAI ships Grok 4.5, the first joint model with Cursor, at half the price of Opus 4.7

Thursday's release is the first frontier model since SpaceXAI closed the $60 billion Cursor acquisition on June 16. Grok 4.5 runs on a 1.5-trillion-parameter MoE trained alongside the Cursor engineering harness, prices at $2 input and $6 output per million tokens, and lands in Grok Build, the SpaceXAI console and every Cursor plan on day one. Elon Musk called it an "Opus-class model, but faster, more token-efficient and lower cost" on a live stream at 09:00 PT. Public availability follows on July 9. The European Union is excluded from the launch pending Digital Services Act review.

Bloomberg · TechCrunch · Axios · Forbes · 2026-07-08 · Source ↗ ·

SpaceXAI released Grok 4.5 on Thursday morning at a live-streamed event from the Palo Alto Cursor office. Bloomberg's Rachel Metz broke the pricing terms during the stream, and TechCrunch's Kyle Wiggers and Axios's Ina Fried both matched with independent confirmations within thirty minutes. Grok 4.5 is the first frontier model shipped by the combined company since SpaceX closed its $60 billion all-stock acquisition of Cursor on June 16, and the model is trained jointly with the Cursor coding harness for tight in-editor integration.

The model runs on SpaceXAI's V9 base architecture at 1.5 trillion parameters in a mixture-of-experts configuration. Pricing lands at $2 per million input tokens and $6 per million output tokens. That undercuts Anthropic's Claude Opus 4.7 by 60 percent on input and 76 percent on output, and undercuts OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol by 60 percent on input and 80 percent on output. Musk framed the release as "an Opus-class model, but faster, more token-efficient and lower cost" and said internal benchmarks put Grok 4.5 "roughly comparable to Opus 4.7, but much faster."

Grok 4.5 was trained with Cursor from the first checkpoint. It is the first model in the world that was designed as an IDE tenant, not a chat tenant.— Elon Musk, SpaceXAI CEO, on the launch live stream

Distribution and the Cursor merge

Day-one availability covers three surfaces: the Grok Build API console, the SpaceXAI web console, and the Cursor editor across every subscription tier including the free plan. Cursor Pro and Cursor Business customers get an eight-month usage allowance at pre-acquisition Cursor pricing while the combined billing stack migrates onto SpaceXAI. The European Union is excluded from launch pending Digital Services Act review; SpaceXAI told The Next Web the model will "arrive when European regulators complete their capability assessment." Public consumer availability at grok.com follows on July 9.

The Cursor acquisition at $60 billion was announced on June 16 and closed inside 22 days, unusually fast for a deal of that size. Cursor co-founder and CEO Michael Truell moves into a President of AI Product role reporting directly to Musk. The acquisition was priced roughly 3x Cursor's last venture mark and about 45x annualised revenue, which puts the transaction inside the same private-market range as Meta's Scale AI purchase in June 2025 and Google's Anthropic Series H participation last November.

Benchmarks and positioning

SpaceXAI published benchmark scores framing Grok 4.5 as "just short of best-in-class" against the current frontier. The company shared internal numbers for coding, terminal use and multi-turn agent work, though independent verification is still pending. Forbes noted Musk's line that "the token efficiency is twice greater than the nearest competing model," which SpaceXAI attributes to a new routing scheme in the MoE that assigns short-context tokens to smaller expert clusters. Cybernews framed the release as the first head-to-head competitor to Claude at the specific price band Anthropic dominates for agentic coding.

The token efficiency is twice greater than the nearest competing model.— Elon Musk, cited by Forbes

Market context

The launch runs into a busy Thursday. OpenAI confirmed the same morning that GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra and Luna would exit the U.S. government's restricted-access window and go public on Thursday afternoon Pacific time. OpenAI also rolled out GPT-Live-1 and GPT-Live-1 mini voice models globally. Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro release slipped to July 17 after a full architectural rebuild off the 2.5 Pro base. Anthropic pushed Claude Cowork to web and mobile with new Microsoft 365 write tools. The four-frontier release day is the tightest overlap since November 2024.

The pricing move is the sharpest part of the story. Grok 4.5 sits at the middle tier of the current frontier stack in raw capability, but at pricing that Anthropic has historically reserved for one tier down. Anthropic's Sonnet 5, launched on June 30, prices at $2 input and $10 output through August 31 on a promotional rate. Grok 4.5 undercuts even that promotional price on output tokens. For agentic coding workloads where output volume dominates the bill, the shift is material.

SpaceXAI made a straightforward move today: it took the Anthropic price band and undercut it while claiming Anthropic-tier capability.— Kyle Wiggers, TechCrunch

What the release means for Cursor

Cursor's product pitch since 2023 has been that the model matters less than the harness. The Grok 4.5 launch is the first material test of that thesis under joint ownership. Cursor now ships a model that other harnesses cannot match, since the model is trained on IDE session data no third party has. Windsurf, Aider and Zed all have Cursor-compatible product surfaces but cannot access the joint training data. Cognition, which competes with Devin through its own harness plus Windsurf acquisition, told Axios that "the harness gap is exactly what we have been building around."

The next test is whether the EU delay narrows. If the Digital Services Act review returns a positive assessment inside eight weeks, Grok 4.5 sits inside every major legal market before Google DeepMind ships Gemini 3.5 Pro on July 17. If the review stretches to Q4, SpaceXAI cedes the entire European enterprise coding market to Anthropic and OpenAI through year-end.

CNBC · Axios · Neowin · 2026-07-08 · Source ↗ ·

GPT-5.6 Sol, Terra and Luna go public Thursday afternoon Pacific, two weeks after the U.S. Department of Commerce restricted early access to a small group of trusted partners. Axios first reported the Commerce sign-off. OpenAI said in its statement that "this kind of government access process should not become the long-term default."

OpenAI confirmed Thursday morning that the GPT-5.6 family will exit the government-restricted access window and go public at 14:00 Pacific. CNBC's Hayden Field reported the timing based on an internal OpenAI staff memo circulated Wednesday night. Axios's Ina Fried added that a Department of Commerce spokesperson confirmed the department "has completed its capability assessment and clears OpenAI to proceed with broader distribution." The two-week window opened on June 26 after OpenAI agreed to a White House request to restrict initial access to "a small group of trusted partners" while the government reviewed cyber and biosecurity risks.

The public release covers all three tiers. Sol, the flagship, prices at $5 input and $30 output per million tokens and targets frontier reasoning and long-horizon agent workloads. Terra prices at $2.50 input and $15 output, positioned as the everyday tier that matches GPT-5.5 on capability at half the price. Luna prices at $1 input and $6 output, targeted at high-throughput consumer applications where latency matters more than reasoning depth. All three become available Thursday through the OpenAI API, ChatGPT Plus and ChatGPT Team, with ChatGPT Enterprise rollout the following Monday.

We do not believe this kind of government access process should become the long-term default. It keeps the best tools from users, developers, enterprises, cyber defenders and global partners who need them.— OpenAI statement to CNBC, July 8, 2026

The Commerce review

The June 26 restriction was the first invocation of the voluntary pre-release testing clause under Trump's June 2 executive order on "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security." Under that order, developers of covered frontier models may submit systems for a 30-day federal review before public release. GPT-5.6 was the first model designated "covered frontier" by the NSA director's classified benchmarking process. Commerce ran the review in coordination with the NSA and the U.S. AI Safety Institute; the Institute told Axios its findings were shared with Commerce on July 3 and that no critical risk was identified inside the review scope.

OpenAI's public statement framed the two-week window as an accommodation, not a template. The company had pushed back publicly on June 26, saying restricted-access releases are "not consistent with the innovation goals of the executive order itself." Sam Altman told employees at a June 30 all-hands that "we agreed to the delay because it was the right thing to do this once, not because we plan to do it again." The Thursday release closes the loop on that commitment.

What launches Thursday

Sol includes a new "Ultra subagent" mode that allows a top-level Sol instance to spawn up to eight parallel subagents on the same task and merge their outputs. Terra ships with the same 200,000-token context window as GPT-5.5 and matches the older model's benchmark scores at half the price. Luna serves at 750 tokens per second on the Cerebras premium tier and targets voice, translation and high-frequency retrieval workloads. Developers who signed up for the restricted-access wave get their tokens grandfathered into their Thursday quota.

OpenAI releases GPT-Live and GPT-Live-1 mini, full-duplex voice models that keep talking while delegating to GPT-5.5

OpenAI · CNBC · U.S. News · MarkTechPost · 2026-07-08 · Source ↗ ·

GPT-Live listens and speaks at the same time, adds backchannel cues like "mhmm" during pauses, and hands complex reasoning to GPT-5.5 Instant without pausing the conversation. Rolls to iOS and Android globally on all paid ChatGPT tiers Thursday. API access remains gated behind a signup form.

OpenAI rolled out GPT-Live-1 and GPT-Live-1 mini for ChatGPT Voice on Thursday, replacing the Advanced Voice Mode stack that had run on a GPT-4o-derived model with a January 2024 knowledge cutoff. The new models are full-duplex: they process incoming audio and generate outgoing audio in the same pass, and the model makes decisions many times per second about whether to speak, keep listening, pause, interrupt or call a tool. That architecture kills the turn-based silence-detection loop that made the prior voice mode read as stilted.

The delegation model is the meaningful architectural change. GPT-Live and GPT-Live-1 mini run their own reasoning pass for conversational back-and-forth but hand complex queries to GPT-5.5 Instant as a background call. Higher reasoning tiers dispatch to GPT-5.5 Thinking with configurable effort. During the delegation call, the voice model continues the conversation with backchannel utterances, filler and paraphrased summaries of what the user just said, so the audio track never dies while the background query returns.

The new model is very impressive. My longest conversation with it lasted a full hour, on a dog walk in Monterey Bay. I still haven't managed to photograph an owl.— Simon Willison, weblog, July 8, 2026

Benchmarks and preference

OpenAI's release included human evaluation results showing GPT-Live-1 and mini were "strongly preferred over Advanced Voice Mode" on turn-taking, interruption handling and conversational naturalness. Automated benchmarks reported substantial gains on GPQA for expert-level science reasoning, strong improvements on BrowseComp for agentic web-search tasks, and superior multi-turn performance on the τ³-Voice Telecom benchmark. The model ships with nine remastered voices and supports live translation via full-duplex timing, weather and stock card display, memory, image and file uploads.

The Willison bug

Simon Willison's preview write-up flagged an early GPT-Live-1 build that would interrupt conversations with inappropriate laughter, most reliably triggered when the topic drifted to the locations of owls. Willison documented one instance where the model laughed for eleven consecutive seconds during a discussion of a Great Horned Owl nest. OpenAI's release-day statement said the issue was patched in the launch build; Willison's follow-up on Thursday confirmed the launch build no longer reproduces the behavior on the same prompts.

GPT-Live continuously processes audio input while it is generating audio output. It makes decisions many times per second about whether to speak, keep listening, pause, interrupt or call a tool.— OpenAI announcement

Availability

The rollout covers ChatGPT users globally on iOS and Android across Plus, Team and Enterprise tiers. Free-tier users get a limited daily quota starting Thursday. API access remains unavailable at launch; OpenAI is accepting signups for a developer preview later in Q3. The launch pricing is bundled inside existing ChatGPT subscriptions rather than metered per minute.

China's Ministry of Industry and Information Technology issued a formal security advisory Thursday ordering domestic developers and enterprises to remove Claude Code from installed environments if they are running versions 2.1.91 through 2.1.196. The MIIT notice alleges the autonomous coding tool contains a "security back-door vulnerability" that transmits sensitive information to a remote server without a user's consent, including user location and identity. Affected users are instructed to either uninstall the tool entirely or upgrade to a patched build outside the flagged range.

CNBC reported the MIIT advisory the same morning. The advisory is the first Chinese state-issued security notice targeting a specific US-origin AI coding tool by version number. Anthropic did not respond to CNBC's Thursday-morning request for comment. Anthropic's release notes for Claude Code v2.1.198, which shipped on July 3, mention "enhanced telemetry controls" without naming a specific vulnerability. The v2.1.198 release also promoted the Chrome extension to GA and added a Notification hook with agent_needs_input and agent_completed events.

Claude Code contains a security back-door vulnerability. The autonomous coding tool can send sensitive information to a remote server without a user's consent, including a user's location and identity.— MIIT security advisory, per CNBC translation

The wider context

The advisory sits inside a broader escalation between US and Chinese AI regulators. In June, US export-control officials pushed Anthropic to suspend Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 globally for nineteen days after research disclosed that models could produce actionable cyber-offensive guidance. Anthropic proposed the Cyber Jailbreak Severity scale co-signed with Amazon, Microsoft and Google earlier this month as an industry-standard scoring framework for exactly the failure mode the MIIT is now flagging.

The specific claim, that Claude Code sends location and identity without consent, is unusual. Claude Code's default posture logs telemetry only after explicit opt-in, and Anthropic publishes the exact fields transmitted. The MIIT notice does not detail the technical mechanism, and no third-party security research has publicly reproduced the claim as of Thursday afternoon. The advisory functions as a de-facto ban for Chinese enterprises regardless of whether the technical claim survives scrutiny, since domestic firms typically follow MIIT notices as compliance floor.

FTC settles the John Deere right-to-repair suit with a ten-year mandate for dealer-parity software access

FTC · Engadget · KCRG · 2026-07-08 · Source ↗ ·

Under the settlement, Deere must provide farmers and independent repair providers with the same software, diagnostic tools and technical resources it currently gives authorized dealers, for ten years under FTC and state oversight. Deere pays $1 million to cover state legal expenses and is barred from retaliating against self-repair customers.

The Federal Trade Commission, together with the attorneys general of Illinois, Arizona, Michigan, Minnesota and Wisconsin, reached a settlement Thursday with Deere & Company ending the January 2025 antitrust suit that alleged Deere unlawfully monopolized the market for repair of Deere agricultural equipment. Under the settlement, Deere must provide farmers and independent repair providers with the same repair resources, including applicable software capabilities, that it currently provides to authorized Deere dealers. The obligation runs for ten years under FTC and plaintiff-state supervision.

The settlement centers on the software and diagnostic tools that Deere embeds in modern agricultural equipment. Since roughly 2015, Deere tractors have shipped with electronic control units that require dealer-only software to reset after a repair, effectively locking independent shops and farmers out of repairs that were previously mechanical. The FTC's January 2025 complaint alleged that gating amounted to monopoly maintenance in the repair market. Deere denied wrongdoing throughout but agreed to the ten-year mandate to end the litigation.

Deere is also barred from discriminating or retaliating against customers who choose to perform their own repairs.— FTC press release, July 8, 2026

Terms

Deere pays $1 million combined to the plaintiff states to cover legal costs. Deere must publish a repair-tools API and maintain public documentation for the software interfaces required to reset control units.
