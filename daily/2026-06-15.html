The Commerce Department took an American frontier model off the market

A Friday 5:21pm ET export-control directive from Howard Lutnick forced Anthropic to suspend Fable 5 and Mythos 5 to every customer in the world, after Amazon researchers briefed Andy Jassy on Thursday and Jassy carried a narrow jailbreak finding into senior administration meetings. Anthropic disputes the framing, calls the bypass "narrow," and flew senior technical staff to Washington over the weekend. David Sacks says the administration asked Amodei to fix the issue or de-deploy and was refused. Mark Carney compares the episode to 2008 ahead of the G7. The European Commission warns the measure must not be discriminatory against allies. It is the first time the US government has pulled an already-deployed commercial AI model out of public service.

Fortune · Anthropic · Axios · 2026-06-14 · Source ↗ ·

Anthropic disabled Fable 5 and Mythos 5 to every customer at 5:21pm Eastern on Friday June 12 after Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick signed a single-page export-control directive that banned the two models from being made available to any foreign national, anywhere — including non-citizen Anthropic employees inside the United States. The company had roughly 90 minutes to comply. Because Anthropic cannot enforce nationality-based access controls in real time across its API, Bedrock, Vertex, Foundry, GitHub Copilot, Harvey, Cursor and consumer-facing surfaces, it cut both models globally rather than try to segment by passport. By Saturday afternoon Fortune's Sharon Goldman published a definitive account tracing the directive to Amazon CEO Andy Jassy, who had relayed concerns from Amazon researchers about a Fable 5 jailbreak to senior administration officials the prior Thursday.

The mechanics of the shutdown sit on top of a longer political dispute. The Pentagon placed Anthropic on a department-wide blacklist three months ago after the company refused to allow the US military to use its models for fully autonomous weapons systems. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth amplified the suspension Saturday with a single-sentence X post that read, "Three months ago, @DeptofWar kicked @AnthropicAI out of our building — forever." Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and White House Cyber Director Sean Cairncross held a call with Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei in the hours before the directive shipped; Bessent reportedly told Amodei directly that he was "making a bad decision." The Lutnick letter warned of "prompt criminal and civil penalties, as provided for by law" for non-compliance with the foreign-national restriction.

"The export restrictions were the last resort after we had begged them for hours to work with us."— White House official, via Politico

What Amazon actually demonstrated

Fortune reports that Amazon researchers used a series of prompts to get a Mythos-class model to surface information about cyberattacks that the model was supposed to keep behind its safety guardrails. The Politico account says the White House solicited Amazon's feedback; Amazon's own spokesperson framed it as standard sharing of security observations between a major cloud provider and the federal government. Anthropic reviewed the demonstration and concluded the technique surfaced "a small number of previously known, minor vulnerabilities" that other widely available models could find too. The company called the bypass "narrow rather than a full jailbreak" in conversations with administration officials. The directive shipped anyway. Anthropic spent Saturday and Sunday placing senior technical staff in Washington for in-person negotiations with the administration aimed at resolving the suspension before Wednesday June 17, when Amodei is scheduled to sit alongside Sam Altman and Demis Hassabis at the G7 working lunch on AI safety in Évian-les-Bains.

"As a leading cloud provider that serves a large number of private and public sector customers, it's not uncommon for governments to seek our counsel on potential security risks."— Amazon spokesperson, to Fortune

The Sacks framing and the China question

Former Trump AI czar David Sacks posted Saturday June 13 that the administration had asked Amodei to either fix the jailbreak or pull Fable 5 from public service, and that Amodei refused. "We asked them to fix the jailbreak or de-deploy the model. They refused. So we had no choice but to issue the export control. We hope this can be resolved quickly," Sacks wrote on X. Semafor reported the same weekend that the directive was issued partly on suspicion that a China-linked group had accessed Mythos 5. Anthropic disputes both points publicly. The company says administration officials did not raise Chinese access as a concern in any of the pre-directive calls, and says it offered remediation paths that the administration declined to discuss. The two accounts are not reconcilable on the public record.

"We believe this is a misunderstanding and are working to restore access as soon as possible."— Anthropic, on X, Friday evening

What is offline

Fable 5 was Anthropic's flagship general-use model, released June 9 at $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens with launch partner integrations at GitHub Copilot, Amazon Bedrock, the Claude Platform on AWS, Microsoft Foundry, Harvey, Cursor and Replit. Mythos 5 was the higher-capability variant deployed to roughly 200 critical-infrastructure partners across 15-plus countries under "Project Glasswing." Both endpoints now return access errors. Customers who built production agent stacks against Fable 5 over the prior weekend have been routing to Claude Opus 4.8 as a fallback; Replit, Cursor and Harvey all confirmed downgrades in the same window. The European Commission has formally opened an inquiry into the discriminatory effect of the restriction on EU users. Anthropic's confidential S-1, filed June 1 at a $965 billion private mark, will need a fresh risk-factor disclosure before any banker can reprice the listing. The first export-controlled American frontier model is also the first frontier model whose IPO path now sits inside an active national-security dispute with the sitting administration.

The directive itself, signed by Howard Lutnick, requires a separate validated license for "any export, re-export, or domestic transfer" of either model to "all destinations worldwide" — a phrasing that effectively forced Anthropic to disable both models globally because the company cannot perform real-time nationality screening across its API and partner surfaces. The Pentagon blacklisted Anthropic three months ago over the company's refusal to allow autonomous-weapons use of its models. "Failure to comply will result in prompt criminal and civil penalties, as provided for by law."— Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick's letter to Dario Amodei

What a remediation could look like

The technical staff in Washington are negotiating two distinct surfaces. One is the narrow bypass Amazon researchers demonstrated on Thursday and the safety-classifier patches Anthropic has been developing since Friday afternoon. The other is the harder operational question of how Anthropic can certify foreign-national exclusion without disabling foreign-national access across an entire product family. Possible structures include a US-only API gateway, an attestation framework built on the Claude Compliance API that Linx Security publicly integrated last week, and a nationality-screening obligation pushed downstream to cloud partners like Amazon Bedrock and Google Vertex. None of these can be implemented in 72 hours. The realistic outcome before Wednesday is a Commerce Department acknowledgement that the narrow bypass has been remediated, a phased restoration of access starting with US-domiciled enterprise customers, and a separate working group on the foreign-national question.

Mark Carney compares the Anthropic shutdown to 2008 ahead of the G7

The Next Web · 2026-06-14 · Source ↗

Speaking to reporters in Ireland on Saturday, Canada's Mark Carney framed the Fable 5 and Mythos 5 suspension as a systemic-concentration warning and used the precise prudential-regulation phrase "model risk." Carney hosts the G7 in Évian-les-Bains starting Monday with Amodei, Altman and Hassabis all confirmed for the working lunch.

Canadian Prime Minister Mark Carney, speaking to reporters during a state visit to Ireland on Saturday June 14, framed the US export-control suspension of Anthropic's Fable 5 and Mythos 5 as a systemic warning about concentration risk in global AI infrastructure. Carney drew a deliberate parallel to the 2008 financial crisis, the precise prudential-regulation analogy that anchored his career as Bank of Canada governor through that crisis and Bank of England governor through Brexit. He used the term "model risk" — the same term banking supervisors apply to a financial institution's over-reliance on a small number of internal models — and argued that the financial-regulation principles of redundancy and diversity should now be applied to AI infrastructure.

The G7 summit in Évian-les-Bains runs Monday June 15 through Wednesday June 17 and is the first time representatives of all three leading US AI labs will sit at the same table with G7 heads of state. The working lunch on Wednesday lists Amodei, Altman and Hassabis on the participant sheet. Carney spent 45 minutes discussing AI with French President Emmanuel Macron during the same week, and signed a bilateral AI cooperation agreement with Ireland's Taoiseach during the visit. Canada's own sovereign-AI build is funded under a CAD 2.3 billion strategy Carney launched June 4.

"The situation we're in collectively right now with Mythos and Fable is something that can happen with over-reliance on certain models."— Mark Carney, Prime Minister of Canada
"Nobody has done anything wrong in this situation. But we will have done something wrong if we just accept this, don't take the lesson, don't build out and diversify."— Mark Carney

Why the analogy lands

The 2008 framing matters because it positions AI infrastructure as a candidate for prudential supervision rather than purely competition or trade policy. The supervisory tools that grew out of the 2008 response — capital buffers, stress tests, resolution authority — were responses to concentration risk and single-point-of-failure dependence inside an interconnected system. Carney's argument is that AI now has the same shape: a small number of frontier providers, deep cross-system dependence, and a regulator who can pull a single thread and unwind enterprise stacks across multiple jurisdictions. The Évian agenda is now overweight model risk because of the Friday shutdown, and Carney told reporters not to expect a "mission accomplished banner" at week's end.
