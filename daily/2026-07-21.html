Moonshot pauses Kimi K3 signups as GPU floor gives way under demand

Five days after Kimi K3 dragged the AI complex through its worst tape of the year, Moonshot AI froze new subscriber signups Monday. Existing subscribers keep access; the company told developers it is bringing capacity online "in batches" as fast as it can find GPUs the export regime lets in. The 2.8-trillion-parameter mixture-of-experts model still holds the Arena Frontend Code top slot at Elo 1,679 against Claude Fable 5 at 1,631 and GPT-5.6 Sol at 1,618. The pause hits with the six-month Hong Kong IPO window already circulating at a $30-billion mark and full open weights due Monday July 27.

South China Morning Post · 2026-07-20 · Source ↗

Moonshot AI paused new subscriptions to its Kimi K3 model Monday morning Beijing time, telling developers on the Kimi app and API console that surging demand had pushed the service "close to the limits of current capacity." Existing subscribers keep access under the message posted on the platform. The company said it is adding compute "as fast as we can" and will reopen new subscription spots in batches. Bloomberg, the South China Morning Post, PYMNTS, Dataconomy, Reuters via Yahoo Finance, TheNextWeb, Invezz and AOL all ran versions of the story within the trading day. Moonshot did not disclose specific numbers on active subscribers, queued requests, or new GPU orders. The pause is the first public admission from a top Chinese lab that shipping a Fable-class model is now bottlenecked less by algorithmic innovation than by physical GPUs the US export regime lets into the country.

"Over the past 48 hours, demand has been close to the limits of Moonshot AI's current capacity. We're adding capacity as fast as we can and will reopen new subscription spots in batches."— Moonshot AI, in-app notice cited by South China Morning Post

What K3 shipped, and why the servers folded

Kimi K3, released Thursday July 16, is a 2.8-trillion-parameter mixture-of-experts model activating 16 of 896 experts per token, giving roughly 50 billion parameters live per query with a 1-million-token context window and native multimodal input. It opened at Elo 1,679 on Arena.ai's Frontend Code leaderboard against Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 at 1,631 and OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol at 1,618, and Artificial Analysis places its aggregate intelligence tier alongside Claude Opus 4.8 and GPT-5.5, one notch behind Fable 5 and Sol. API pricing was set at $3 per million cache-miss input tokens, $0.30 on cache-hit, and $15 per million output tokens, roughly one-third of Anthropic's forthcoming $10 input and $50 output metered rate for Fable 5. The consumer Kimi app climbed to the top of the Chinese iOS App Store productivity chart over the weekend, a spot Deepseek held for most of Q1 2025. By Monday, response latency on the Kimi app had ticked up meaningfully and API 429 rate-limit responses were common. The company's public-facing status page acknowledged degraded throughput before the subscription pause landed.

The export regime as a bottleneck

The Kimi K3 launch and the subscription pause together sharpen a point that has been implicit in Chinese frontier-model announcements all year: the constraint on scaling inference to public demand is not model quality, it is Nvidia H200/H100 supply. Moonshot is understood to run its production inference stack on a mixture of stockpiled H100s bought before the October 2023 export controls, downgraded H20 units allowed by the current rules, and domestic Huawei Ascend 910C and 950 accelerators cutting in incrementally through 2026. Domestic supply, even at Huawei's 8,192-Ascend Atlas 950 SuperPoD scale unveiled at WAIC last Friday, cannot yet compensate for API-scale traffic. Multiple outlets covering the pause tied it directly to the "hardware constraints from US export controls on Nvidia chips," making explicit the two-front problem: any Chinese lab that ships a Fable-class product now hits the compute wall before the demand wall.

Rerating the cap table

The pause lands mid-flight through a shareholder resolution circulating over the weekend seeking approval for a Hong Kong listing within six months at a $30-billion-plus post-money mark, roughly 7.5 times Moonshot's late-2025 valuation of about $4 billion. Goldman Sachs and China International Capital Corporation are both in talks to lead the offering. Annual recurring revenue climbed from $200 million in April to $300 million in June on Kimi K3 consumption before the model even released; the compute crunch now bounds the pace at which Moonshot can convert that demand into billed revenue. A parallel private round, roughly $2 billion at the same mark with Alibaba, Tencent and China Mobile as anchor investors, is expected to close before the IPO filing. DeepSeek is running the parallel Shanghai STAR Market track at approximately 500 billion yuan, roughly $74 billion post-money, with a filing targeted before year-end.

"Kimi K3 is so popular Moonshot ran out of GPUs and paused new sign-ups."— TheNextWeb headline, 20 July 2026

The Fable-price shock and Anthropic's reversal

K3 pricing at $3 input / $15 output has already forced a reversal from Anthropic, which on Sunday announced that from Monday July 20 Claude Fable 5 would be permanently bundled into every Max and Team Premium plan at 50 percent of the standard usage base, while Pro and Team Standard revert to $10 input / $50 output metered pricing with a one-time $100 credit. The path Anthropic walked over 48 hours, from Fable 5 as an API-only premium primitive to a bundled Max default, is the sharpest live example of open-weight competitive pressure moving pricing on the frontier tier. Bank of America pegged K3's cost per task at roughly half of Sol's.

Weights open Monday July 27

Moonshot has committed to publishing full open weights for Kimi K3 on Hugging Face on Monday July 27, with an intermediate technical-preview drop scheduled for Wednesday July 22 and a full technical report on architecture, training data, and evaluations releasing alongside the weights. Even Elo-parity open weights would land in a market where DeepSeek V4 Pro, Alibaba's freshly previewed Qwen 3.8 Max at 2.4 trillion parameters, GLM-5.2 from Z.ai, and MiniMax M3 already crowd the top of the open-weight leaderboards. The subscription pause underlines what the July 27 release will not fix: enterprise self-hosting on H200 or MI400 clusters at 2.8T parameters requires infrastructure Chinese customers largely do not own, and Nvidia infrastructure Western buyers can access through native cloud channels. K3 open-weight downloads will land inside Western developer workflows before the Kimi app can serve them at scale, an inversion of the usual model-release pattern where consumer products and API access precede weight releases by weeks or months.
