Anthropic crosses in front of OpenAI: $965 billion, Opus 4.8, Mythos, and Karpathy in one month

A $13 billion primary at $965 billion post-money closed Friday, May 29. Opus 4.8 went live Tuesday, May 26 with the highest SWE-bench Verified score on the public leaderboard. Andrej Karpathy joined the pre-training team the prior Monday. Mythos public release was telegraphed May 25. Four moves inside ten trading days reordered the frontier-lab ledger and set the bar for the rest of 2026.

Fortune · CNBC · Axios · TechCrunch · 2026-05-19 — 2026-05-29 · Source ↗ ·

May 2026 reordered the frontier-lab leaderboard inside ten trading days. Anthropic closed a $13 billion primary fundraise at a $965 billion post-money valuation on Friday, May 29, becoming the most valuable private AI lab and overtaking OpenAI for the first time. Iconiq Capital led, with Fidelity, Lightspeed and Altimeter participating; secondary tender activity pushed effective valuation marks above $1 trillion in the days after the close. The round followed Claude Opus 4.8's Tuesday, May 26 general availability, four days after Anthropic published its public-release plan for "Mythos-class" cyber-capable models, and ten days after Andrej Karpathy announced he had joined the pre-training team under Nick Joseph.

Each move stood up on its own. Together they shifted the lab ledger. OpenAI's last marked valuation sits at $850 billion from its March secondary; xAI's last raise valued it at $200 billion; Google's AI work runs inside a parent worth $2.4 trillion but does not pencil out separately. Anthropic's $965 billion mark is the highest private valuation in technology, ahead of SpaceX at $400 billion and ByteDance at $400 billion. The company's Q1 disclosure put annualized run-rate revenue at $44 billion, eighty times the prior year. Customers spending more than $1 million a year doubled to over a thousand in two months. The new capital is committed almost entirely to compute, against the AWS and Google Cloud contracts coming online through 2027.

Opus 4.8 and what it changes

Claude Opus 4.8 went live Tuesday across the Claude API, Claude Code, Cursor and Anthropic's first-party products. Reported scores put Opus 4.8 at 82.4% on SWE-bench Verified, the highest on the public leaderboard, ahead of GPT-5.5 at 78.6%, Gemini 3.5 Flash agent harness at 76.2% Terminal-Bench 2.1, and Qwen 3.7 Max at 60.6 SWE-Pro. Pricing held flat at $15 per million input and $75 per million output tokens. The release added constitutional-AI improvements on tool-use refusal rates and a wider extended-thinking budget. Same release: Mythos-class capabilities, behind a new "containment" wrapper Anthropic says is required before any wider deployment.

"At present, no company — including Anthropic — has developed safeguards strong enough to prevent such models from being misused."— Anthropic, Mythos public-release announcement, May 25

Mythos and Project Glasswing

Anthropic said it intends to expand Project Glasswing, the controlled program through which select governments and enterprises currently access Mythos, before opening Mythos-class capability more broadly. Mythos identified 6,202 high-or-critical vulnerabilities across more than 1,000 open-source projects in its preview run; of 1,752 high-or-critical findings reviewed, 90.6% were valid. A critical wolfSSL flaw discovered by Mythos affects billions of devices and enables certificate forgery; only 75 of 530 reported high-or-critical bugs have been patched as of the announcement. The roadmap names "near future" as the target for wider release without committing to a date. The EU told CNBC on May 29 it was seeking to intensify talks with the U.S. on advanced cyber AI models in the wake of Mythos's preview disclosures.

Karpathy's mandate

Karpathy started at Anthropic the week of May 18 under pre-training lead Nick Joseph. Reporting from TechCrunch and CNBC says his mandate is a new team using Claude itself to accelerate pre-training research on the next model. He paused Eureka Labs, the education startup he founded in 2024, to take the role. The hire matters two ways. Karpathy is one of a handful of researchers who has built both the theory and the production-scale training systems behind a frontier-quality model, having led GPT pre-training at OpenAI and the autopilot stack at Tesla. And his departure removes from OpenAI's orbit one of the most visible original-eleven names, two months after Ilya Sutskever's SSI raised at $32 billion and three months after John Schulman's exit from Mira Murati's lab.

"I've joined Anthropic. I think the next few years at the frontier of LLMs will be especially formative. I am very excited to join the team here and get back to R&D."— Andrej Karpathy, May 19, on X

What the convergence means

The cluster matters because each piece reinforces the others. Opus 4.8 gives Anthropic the top frontier-model SWE-bench position. The $965 billion mark lets it secure the AWS and Google Cloud compute draw without further dilution. Karpathy gives it the rare combination of theory and infrastructure required to keep widening the model-quality lead. Mythos turns the lab into a defense customer in addition to a developer customer. By the close of May, Anthropic has the strongest revenue, the highest valuation, the top model on SWE-bench, the most credible cyber-capability story, and a fresh founding-tier engineer. OpenAI retains the larger user base, the Stargate infrastructure commitment, and the closer White House relationship under Greg Brockman. The two companies' valuation and revenue gap is the smallest it has been since Anthropic was founded, and Anthropic crossed in front in May.

"The capital is committed to compute. The 2027 cloud commitments require it. The model quality requires it. The customer pipeline requires it."— Dario Amodei, Anthropic CEO, on the Fortune funding call

Risks remain. The $965 billion mark embeds a roughly 22x forward revenue multiple on the Q1 disclosed ARR, on the higher end of frontier-lab marks. The Mythos public-release plan is contingent on safeguards Anthropic itself says do not yet exist. Karpathy's mandate is research; the production pre-training stack stays with Nick Joseph's organization, and a meaningful team has to form around the new hire. Opus 4.8's lead on SWE-bench is on a benchmark the field increasingly distrusts, with VentureBeat reporting this same month that buyers' deployment outcomes do not track benchmark rankings. None of those caveats undoes the month. They set the bar for what the next two quarters have to look like.

The month in AI

May was the month the frontier-lab order changed. Anthropic crossed in front of OpenAI on valuation at $965 billion, shipped Opus 4.8 with the top SWE-bench Verified score on the public leaderboard, queued Mythos-class cyber tooling for wider release, and pulled Andrej Karpathy out of OpenAI's founding-engineer alumni pool. Inside the same calendar, Google I/O 2026 landed Gemini 3.5 Flash GA, the Spark personal-agent product, Antigravity 2.0, Managed Agents API and TPU 8t / 8i. SoftBank booked up to €75 billion to build 5 gigawatts of French data centers. OpenAI's internal reasoning model disproved an 80-year-old Erdős conjecture with a 125-page algebraic-number-theory proof verified by Tim Gowers and Noga Alon. Mistral Large 3 shipped at 675 billion parameters under Apache 2.0. Cognition closed $1 billion at $26 billion. Trump scrapped the 90-day frontier-model AI executive order hours before signing.

The product-market-fit case for frontier labs landed on the books. Anthropic disclosed $44 billion ARR; Cognition's run-rate jumped thirteenfold in a year to roughly $400 million heading into the $1 billion raise; MiniMax filed for a Shanghai IPO on $300 million ARR doubled in two months; OpenAI's Q1 revenue passed $25 billion; Fireworks AI moved into talks at $15 billion. Simon Willison's May 27 reading was that the two leading labs had crossed the durable-PMF threshold and the next 18 months are about scaling, not searching. Three days later the WSJ's tokenmaxxing piece showed the other side of the curve: Uber burned its 2026 token allocation on Claude Code in four months, Amazon killed its Kiro Rank leaderboard after employees gamed it, Microsoft cut Claude Code subscriptions across several divisions, Salesforce now expects a $300 million Anthropic bill this year, and METR had to abandon a planned productivity RCT because coders refuse to work without AI. The abundance era of internal AI usage met the FY budget process, and the FY budget process is winning.

Open weights kept closing the closed-lab gap. Mistral Large 3 shipped at 675B parameters with 41B active under Apache 2.0. Cursor Composer 2.5, built on Moonshot's Kimi K2.5 checkpoint with textual-feedback RL and a 25x synthetic-task expansion, matched Opus 4.7 and GPT-5.5 at one-tenth the API cost. DeepSeek V4 Pro's MIT-licensed weights continued setting the price floor at $0.30 per million output tokens with 80.6% SWE-bench Verified. The price-quality gap between top closed models and best open weights now sits inside a 6% benchmark spread on the public agentic suites and a 30-50x pricing gap in the wrong direction for the closed labs. Qwen 3.7 Max landed as Alibaba's first agent-grade closed-weight Max-tier model, signaling a deliberate two-track program: open weights for research, closed weights for the revenue line.

Federal AI rules collapsed and state rules accelerated. Trump postponed the 90-day pre-release review executive order hours before signing on May 21 after David Sacks, Mark Zuckerberg and Elon Musk objected; the framework is dead with no replacement. California's 30-bill AI package cleared the May 29 crossover deadline with 28 of 30 bills advancing; Colorado HB 1263 moved to Polis with civil penalties of $25,000 per chatbot-safety violation; Illinois sent a five-bill AI package to Pritzker including the Illinois Frontier Model Safety Act. The Bureau of Industry and Security closed the China-subsidiary loophole on Nvidia Blackwell, Rubin and AMD MI350x exports May 31, the most aggressive U.S. export posture change since October 2022. OpenAI published a Frontier Governance Framework on May 29 designed to align with California SB 53 and EU AI Act obligations. CNN became the ninth publisher suing Perplexity for unauthorized scraping. The Leading the Future super PAC, funded by Greg Brockman to argue for federal preemption, now has no federal framework to defend.

Physical infrastructure took the back-half of the month. SoftBank's €75 billion French commitment, Project Jupiter's switch to solid-oxide fuel cells for its 2.8-gigawatt New Mexico OpenAI campus, the WSJ's $26 billion 2025 physical-AI venture tally, Samsung-SK hynix-Micron's combined $3 trillion memory market cap, and Meta's internal hardware memo (Ray-Ban refresh in June, $4,000 AR glasses later in the year, AI pendant for 2027, Wearables for Work unit) reset the AI-infrastructure conversation around watts and atoms rather than tokens. SpaceX took the largest single Golden Dome contract at $4.16 billion for the space-based tracking layer. Nvidia teased a 20-core Arm + Blackwell N1X consumer CPU at Computex. Apple slipped its AI glasses to late 2027. Foundation deployed Phantom MK-1 humanoid robots to Ukraine on a $24 million Pentagon contract. The month closed with battlefield AI politics live: USSOCOM commander Frank Bradley broke with Hegseth publicly, and the Pentagon dropped Anthropic as a battlefield-AI vendor on the same news cycle.

Mistral published Large 3 in mid-May as the first permissively-licensed open-weights model at frontier scale in 2026. The architecture is a sparse mixture of experts at 675 billion total parameters with 41 billion active per forward pass; training ran on 3,000 NVIDIA H200 GPUs. Both base and instruction-tuned variants ship under Apache 2.0 with no commercial restrictions. Large 3 is Mistral's first MoE since the seminal Mixtral series from 2023.

The release sits inside a wider Mistral push. Medium 3.5 shipped on May 2 as a 128B dense model under a modified MIT license, marketed for self-hosting on as few as four GPUs and tuned for "agent harnesses."
