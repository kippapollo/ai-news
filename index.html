This week's briefing · Weekly · 2026-W27

Qualcomm pays $3.92B for Modular and names Meta anchor; Anthropic accuses Qwen of 28.8M Claude exchanges; Lutnick lifts the Mythos 5 ban for 100 institutions.

The week's center of gravity shifted from the model layer to the compute layer. Qualcomm closed Investor Day with a $3.92 billion all-stock acquisition of Chris Lattner's Modular, named Meta as the multi-year anchor for a 250-core Dragonfly C1000 server CPU, and lifted fiscal-2029 non-handset revenue guidance to $40 billion from $22 billion. Anthropic's June 10 letter to the Senate Banking Committee, surfaced June 25, named Alibaba's Qwen lab as running 28.8 million Claude conversations through roughly 25,000 fraudulent accounts to distill Mythos Preview, the largest known distillation attack Anthropic has documented. Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick spent two weeks negotiating a partial lift of the June 12 export-control suspension on Mythos 5 for roughly 100 trusted US institutions on Annex A; Fable 5 stays globally offline. OpenAI previewed GPT-5.6 in three sibling tiers (Sol at $5/$30, Terra at $2.50/$15, Luna at $1/$6 per million tokens) and restricted access to about twenty partners at the Trump administration's request, with chief global affairs officer Chris Lehane telling TechCrunch the trusted-partner gate "shouldn't become the long-term default." Google promoted computer use from a standalone Gemini 2.5 model to a native tool inside Gemini 3.5 Flash at 78.4 on OSWorld-Verified for one-fifth the GPT-5.5 token price. Micron printed $41.45 billion in fiscal Q3 revenue with the stock crossing a $1.2 trillion market cap and signed a four-pillar HBM, SSD, architecture co-design and Series H pact with Anthropic. CNBC's Friday feature called the resulting memory squeeze an "absolute existential crisis" for smaller device makers, with Apple and Microsoft hiking iPad, Mac and Xbox prices and citing the shortage directly. Mistral OCR 4 graduated to GA with 170 languages, in-VPC deployment and a 72 percent win rate over Google Document AI. Salesforce launched Agentforce Commerce with Shopper, Buyer and Merchant agents and native ChatGPT, Google AI Mode and Gemini integrations, with 78 of the Top 2000 North American retailers committed. Epic put Emmie, Penny and Art into production across 85 percent of its hospital base. Sunrun, Tesla and Renew Home formalized a 16.8 gigawatt virtual power plant aimed at hyperscaler AI campuses. Inside 24 hours Sakana AI shipped Fugu and Beijing's 360 unveiled Tulongfeng and Yitianzhen, both pitched at customers who do not want their AI access running through US export approval. Polymarket's $21.99 million end-of-June best-AI-model market closed with Anthropic at 99 percent.

What is Apollo AI News?

Apollo AI News publishes daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly and annual editions on what shifted in AI. Each edition is written to be read end-to-end on the page itself, no clicking through to source articles required.

Every story carries one or two category tags drawn from a fixed taxonomy. The filter bar in each edition's masthead scopes the page to a single tag so a reader can read only the funding, only the policy, or only the tech.

The site is generated and published automatically by a Claude Opus 4.7 agent that searches the web, reads each source article, and writes the summaries in its own words. No prose comes from a template, and no story appears without a real fetched source.
