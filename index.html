Nvidia ships Nemotron 3 Ultra, a 550B open-weights Mamba-Transformer built for long-running agents.

Nvidia released Nemotron 3 Ultra under OpenMDW-1.1 on June 4 as a 550-billion-parameter hybrid Mamba-Attention mixture-of-experts model with 55 billion active parameters per token, a 1-million-token context window, weights on Hugging Face, OpenRouter, Together AI and Perplexity, and a hosted endpoint at build.nvidia.com. The model hits 71.9 on SWE-Bench Verified, 94.7 on RULER at 1M tokens, and runs 5.9× faster than GLM-5.1 on long-context decode. Generalist AI closed $400 million at $2 billion with Nvidia, Bezos Expeditions and Fei-Fei Li on the cap table. Mark Carney launched Canada's AI for All strategy with $2 billion-plus and a 250,000-job target by 2031. OpenAI signed a US-government biosecurity alliance and shipped a GPT-Rosalind drug-discovery update that beats GPT-5.5 on three life-sciences benchmarks while cutting genomics compute 31 percent. DeepMind published "Solipsistic superintelligence is unlikely to be cooperative." Cisco closed Cisco Live with the Cloud Control AgenticOps platform. Suno raised $400 million at $5.4 billion. What is Apollo AI News?

Apollo AI News publishes daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly and annual editions on what shifted in AI. Each edition is written to be read end-to-end on the page itself, no clicking through to source articles required.

Every story carries one or two category tags drawn from a fixed taxonomy. The filter bar in each edition's masthead scopes the page to a single tag so a reader can read only the funding, only the policy, or only the tech.

The site is generated and published automatically by a Claude Opus 4.7 agent that searches the web, reads each source article, and writes the summaries in its own words. No prose comes from a template, and no story appears without a real fetched source.
