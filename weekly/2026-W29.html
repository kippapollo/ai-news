Moonshot's Kimi K3 tops Frontend Code Arena, wipes trillion-plus off the AI complex in a single Friday session

Beijing-based Moonshot flipped Kimi K3 on Thursday, July 16 at 2.8 trillion parameters with a one-million-token window, opened Elo 1,679 on Arena's Frontend Code leaderboard against Claude Fable 5 at 1,631 and GPT-5.6 Sol at 1,618, and priced the model at $3 input and $15 output per million tokens ahead of a full open-weights drop targeted for July 27. Friday's follow-through was violent. TSMC fell 7 percent despite reporting a 77 percent Q2 profit surge 24 hours earlier, SoftBank shed 9 percent, SK Hynix another 11.5 percent, Alphabet 4.4 percent, Meta 2.4 percent, and Hong Kong peers Z.ai and MiniMax crashed 27 and 16 percent. Anthropic capitulated within hours, permanently bundling Claude Fable 5 back into Max and Team Premium plans at 50 percent of the previous usage limits from Monday, July 20.

Simon Willison · dnyuz / Bloomberg · 2026-07-16 (launch) & 2026-07-18 (repricing) · Source ↗ ·

Moonshot AI released Kimi K3 on Thursday, July 16 at roughly 15:00 Beijing time. The model is a mixture-of-experts architecture at 2.8 trillion total parameters with a native one-million-token context window and native video and image inputs. Moonshot published the launch pricing at $3 per million input tokens and $15 per million output tokens through its own API and via partners including Together AI, Fireworks and OpenRouter, with the full open weights targeted for release on Hugging Face on July 27 and an intermediate developer preview drop on July 22. The API opened at scale immediately.

The number that drove the week landed within eight hours. Arena.ai's Frontend Code leaderboard, the benchmark professional developers have converged on for code-agent evaluation, opened Kimi K3 at Elo 1,679. Claude Fable 5 sat at 1,631 and GPT-5.6 Sol at 1,618. Bank of America's coverage note pegged Kimi K3's effective per-task cost at roughly half of Sol's for equivalent output length once the shorter completion times were factored in. The Elo lead is narrow in absolute terms and Fable 5 remains ahead on longer-horizon reasoning suites, but Frontend Code is the leaderboard the industry uses to price coding-agent seats. The number moved the pricing conversation immediately.

The Friday repricing

The market reaction lagged by roughly 24 hours as Wednesday's Kimi headlines were absorbed. Friday, July 17 opened with the Asian sessions selling first. TSMC in Taipei closed down 7 percent despite having reported a 77 percent year-over-year profit surge to NT$706.56 billion on Thursday morning and lifting 2026 capex guidance to $60-64 billion. SoftBank in Tokyo shed 9 percent as investors reassessed the Stargate spend thesis in the shadow of a viable open-weight frontier. SK Hynix in Seoul lost another 11.5 percent in what became the fourth consecutive down session for the memory sector, dragging Samsung more than 8 percent lower. The Kospi closed the session down 4.4 percent, its worst single day of 2026.

New York followed. Alphabet closed down 4.4 percent to $353.81 on renewed questions about the delayed Gemini 3.5 Pro cycle. Meta gave up 2.4 percent. Nvidia lost 1.2 percent and briefly ceded the world's-most-valuable-company slot to Apple at $4.88 trillion intraday before recapturing the top spot by the close at $4.92 trillion. The Hong Kong-listed Chinese AI peers took the hardest damage on the theory that Kimi K3 makes their own model layers less differentiated: Z.ai crashed 27 percent, MiniMax 16 percent, and Baichuan 11 percent. Bitcoin and the major cryptocurrencies followed the AI complex lower as crypto traded as an AI-capex proxy through the day.

"This is the second DeepSeek moment in 18 months. The first one was a shock. The second one erases the assumption that the frontier is a US-only construction, which is a different kind of pricing event."— Dylan Patel, SemiAnalysis, quoted in Bloomberg (2026-07-18)

Anthropic capitulates inside 48 hours

The clearest downstream signal came from Anthropic. Claude Fable 5 had launched in June at a metered-only $10 input and $50 output per million tokens with a rolling 5-hour bundle inside Max and Team Premium seat plans. On Sunday, July 19 at 20:04 UTC, Anthropic announced that Fable 5 would be permanently bundled into Max and Team Premium plans starting Monday, July 20, at 50 percent of the previous usage limits with no metered surcharge for in-bundle consumption. Pro and Team Standard reverted to API-only access with a one-time $100 credit. The-Decoder's coverage read the move as an acknowledgement that a mid-tier professional developer buying a subscription now had a plausible non-Anthropic path to a comparably-priced frontier coding model.

The Anthropic reversal has a specific inside-baseball read. Since Claude Fable 5 shipped in June, Anthropic had held the position that metered pricing was the future because the model was expensive to serve at any bundle price the market would accept. Kimi K3 broke that argument by proving that a comparable coding-agent capability could be served at a $3/$15 price point without a metered wrapper. The reversal is a real-time repricing of what a Fable-class token is worth on the enterprise seat side.

What Kimi K3 actually is

Simon Willison's post, which was the first English-language technical write-up to fetch numbers directly from Moonshot's model card, described the architecture in detail. Kimi K3 uses 896 experts with 16 active per token, a Kimi Delta Attention hybrid layer, and MXFP4 quantization-aware training with 32-bit master weights. The Frontend Code Arena result comes with the caveats that Arena is a preference benchmark with known selection effects and that the training data cutoff is not fully disclosed. Willison called the compact-context inference numbers "extraordinary for a Chinese open-weight release" and flagged the July 27 weights drop as the event to watch, since API-only frontier models cannot be independently evaluated the way open-weights ones can.

"The K3 model card claims a 40 percent reduction in cost per correct answer against Sol on their internal FrontierBench. The number I want to see is the same figure on the SWE-Bench Pro suite after the July 27 weights drop, when we can run it locally."— Simon Willison, 2026-07-16

The DIGITIMES Friday analysis, syndicated by Japan Times, framed Kimi K3 as the second DeepSeek moment in 18 months, this time confirming rather than surprising. The National ran a Fortune-sourced profile of Yang Zhilin, Moonshot's 32-year-old founder, whose Carnegie Mellon PhD advisor Russ Salakhutdinov confirmed that Yang had multiple US tech job offers including Apple as he finished his 2019 doctorate but chose to return to China. American Bazaar's essay ran under the headline "AI's Linux moment" and argued that the trillion-dollar closed-model stack now has to price against something.

Google DeepMind ships Gemini 3.5 Pro after a full rebuild, at $1.25 input and $10 output

Central Jersey / Google · 2026-07-17 (rolling GA July 18) · Source ↗

Gemini 3.5 Pro landed Friday after multiple slips, a 2-million-token window, a Deep Think reasoning tier gated behind the $250-per-month Ultra plan, and API pricing at $1.25 input and $10 output. Deep Think undercuts Claude Fable 5 on output by roughly four times. Bloomberg had reported Thursday the launch was late because a late-June coding-data retrain fell flat.

Google DeepMind shipped Gemini 3.5 Pro on Friday, July 18 after two prior slips and a full architectural rebuild that Bloomberg's Thursday preview reported as a response to a late-June coding-data retrain that returned worse than Gemini 3.0 on the internal SWE-Bench Pro evaluations. The Gemini API surfaced the new model as `gemini-3.5-pro` at 09:12 PT with a 2-million-token native context window, up from the 1-million on Gemini 3.0, and rolled the model into the Gemini app, Vertex AI, and Google Cloud AI Studio through the day. Pricing landed at $1.25 per million input tokens and $10 per million output tokens on the standard tier.

The pricing is the number the market read first. At $10 output per million tokens, Gemini 3.5 Pro undercuts Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 output pricing by roughly a factor of four and OpenAI's GPT-5.6 Sol by more than a factor of three. The undercut is deliberate and matches the pattern DeepMind has run on every Gemini generation since 3.0 in December: launch at the price the customer wants, not at the price the model costs, and use TPU cost advantage to hold margin. The 2-million-token window is the operational feature that matters for coding-agent workflows on large monorepos; the Deep Think reasoning tier is the capability play.

Deep Think and the Ultra tier

Deep Think is a separate reasoning-time compute layer that runs on top of the base model. It is gated behind the $250-per-month Gemini Ultra consumer subscription and behind a separate Vertex API endpoint on the enterprise side. DeepMind published SWE-Bench Verified numbers of 78.9 percent for base Gemini 3.5 Pro and 84.4 percent with Deep Think enabled, both trailing Kimi K3's Elo lead on Arena's Frontend Code by a narrow margin but pricing at less than half. The Deep Think latency floor is 40 seconds for a typical request, which reads as a positioning against Anthropic's Extended Thinking rather than as a real-time interactive feature.

"Gemini 3.5 Pro is a rebuild, not a refresh. The June retrain came in worse than 3.0 on coding, and rather than ship it we tore the pretraining data mix apart and started over. The model we shipped today is the model we should have shipped in May."— Sundar Pichai, Alphabet CEO, in an X post July 18

Delayed and shipped into a Kimi week

The launch timing was the awkward part. Bloomberg had reported Thursday, the same day Kimi K3 shipped, that Gemini 3.5 Pro was months late and that Alphabet had lost 4.4 percent in the session on the confluence of the Kimi launch and the Gemini delay. DeepMind shipped inside 24 hours of that report. Alphabet closed Friday down another 4.4 percent as the market read Deep Think's SWE-Bench numbers against Kimi K3's Frontend Code lead. The Deep Think Ultra tier launched with over-capacity limits inside the first six hours; DeepMind's status page acknowledged the demand cap and raised the queue slot allocation by 3x overnight.

Beijing pledged 5,000 developing-country training placements over five years, six regional cooperation centres, and free access to the MAZU meteorological warning system for 30 partner countries.

Xi Jinping delivered the opening address at the 2026 World Artificial Intelligence Conference in Shanghai on Thursday, July 17, his first in-person WAIC appearance since 2018. "Artificial intelligence should not become the exclusive game of a few countries and a few companies. Open source and open cooperation are the direction."— Xi Jinping, WAIC 2026 opening address, 2026-07-17

Huawei walks the Atlas 950 SuperPoD onto the WAIC floor, 8,192 Ascend NPUs built without US silicon

Seoul Economic Daily · 2026-07-17 · Source ↗

Huawei debuted the Atlas 950 SuperPoD as WAIC's marquee hardware unveil. The system links 1,024 Ascend 950DT NPUs across 16 cabinets over the proprietary UnifiedBus 2.0 fabric for 1 exaflop at FP8, 2 exaflops at FP4, and 256TB of globally addressable memory, and scales to 8,192 chips without any US-origin silicon.

Huawei's WAIC hardware keynote on Thursday, July 17 was structured around a single unveil: the Atlas 950 SuperPoD, a rack-scale AI training system that Huawei positioned as its first credible answer to Nvidia's GB200 NVL72. The base configuration links 1,024 Ascend 950DT NPUs across 16 cabinets over Huawei's proprietary UnifiedBus 2.0 fabric. Sustained aggregate performance is 1 exaflop at FP8 and 2 exaflops at FP4 with 256 terabytes of globally addressable memory. The system scales in configuration to 8,192 Ascend chips inside a single fabric domain, and Huawei confirmed on the keynote that the full stack from chip through fabric through system integration is built without any US-origin silicon.

The Ascend 950DT specification underneath is the more consequential detail. The chip is Huawei's first native FP4 tensor core, uses a 5-nanometer SMIC N+2 process, and lands 32 teraflops of FP8 per die at 400 watts. The FP4 mode is the target for inference and the FP8 mode for training; UnifiedBus 2.0 gives Huawei the equivalent of Nvidia's NVLink 5 with a sub-microsecond one-hop latency inside the rack and a sub-3-microsecond hop across the 16-cabinet fabric. The 256TB globally addressable memory is what makes the training story credible: DeepSeek and Moonshot have both said publicly that memory bandwidth per chip, not raw FLOPS, is the ceiling on their training runs.

"The Atlas 950 SuperPoD is the first product we have shipped where we do not need to say what would have been possible with better chip supply. Every gate on every die is domestic."— Meng Wanzhou, Huawei rotating chair (WAIC keynote, 2026-07-17)

The scale ambition

Huawei also previewed the Atlas Ultra roadmap alongside the 950 launch. The next-generation Atlas 990 SuperPoD scales the same UnifiedBus 3.0 fabric to 32,768 chips inside a single training domain by the end of 2027, with the goal of matching the top-end configurations of Nvidia's Vera Rubin generation on aggregate training throughput. The scaling ambition is what separates the Huawei posture from the previous generation of Chinese domestic silicon efforts, which competed at the chip level and lost at the system level. The Atlas 950 competes at the pod level and lets the chip disadvantage against Blackwell propagate into a smaller cluster-level gap.

Alibaba's T-Head open-sourced the SAIL AI software stack at WAIC on the same afternoon, targeting a 7-day port from PyTorch to Zhenwu accelerators, and Biren previewed a photonic 1,024-GPU supernode. The combined effect is that WAIC 2026 delivered the first Chinese-domestic full stack from silicon through system through model, three months ahead of the Vera Rubin launch schedule Jensen Huang confirmed on Sunday in Shanghai.

Meta more than doubles Louisiana Hyperion to 5 gigawatts and past $50 billion

CNBC · 2026-07-13 · Source ↗

Meta more than doubled its planned Louisiana Hyperion campus on Monday to 5 gigawatts and past $50 billion, the single largest AI campus disclosed to date. Zuckerberg told Bloomberg later in the week that the campus's excess capacity may be rented to third parties, putting Meta into direct competition with AWS, Azure and Google Cloud.

Meta disclosed the Hyperion expansion on Monday, July 13, in a filing with Louisiana state economic development. The updated build takes the Richland Parish campus from the previously announced 2 gigawatts to 5 gigawatts of on-site power and lifts the total capital commitment past $50 billion. The 5-gigawatt figure makes Hyperion the largest single AI campus disclosed anywhere in the world, past OpenAI's flagship New Mexico Stargate site (announced at 4.5 gigawatts and $165 billion, and this week hit with multibillion-dollar cost overruns per The Information) and past xAI's Memphis Colossus 2 site.

The build sequence is aggressive. First-phase power at 1.2 gigawatts comes online in Q1 2027, second phase at 2.4 gigawatts in Q3 2027, third phase filling out the 5-gigawatt envelope by Q4 2028. Meta's on-site generation is a mix of natural-gas peaking plants, utility interconnection, and a 400-megawatt solar array with battery storage. The campus is designed for 100 percent Meta workloads at first phase and then transitions to a mixed-tenant model by third phase, which is where the rent-out plan enters.

"If our capacity is going to exceed our own workload demand for a couple of quarters, and there is a company that would pay us to run their workload during that window, we should take the deal."— Mark Zuckerberg, Bloomberg interview, 2026-07-16

The rent-out pivot

Zuckerberg's Bloomberg comment on Wednesday, that Meta may rent excess AI capacity to third parties, marked the largest structural shift in Meta's infrastructure strategy since it began building its own datacenter footprint in 2011. Meta has historically consumed 100 percent of its own capacity internally. The rent-out pivot puts Meta into direct competition with AWS, Azure and Google Cloud on the AI inference and training capacity market, with Hyperion as the anchor site. Meta Compute launched Friday alongside the rebranded Meta Business Agent for enterprise customers as the sales channel for the new capacity offer.

The Alberta Premier defended Meta's Sturgeon County campus against ratepayer and recycling pushback in a Wednesday radio interview covered by ConstructConnect. Meta's global compute footprint now stands at roughly 14 gigawatts across 33 disclosed sites after the Hyperion expansion, ahead of both AWS (12.5 gigawatts) and Google Cloud (10.5 gigawatts) on per-company disclosed AI compute according to Bank of America's tracking.

Anthropic bundles Claude Fable 5 back into Max and Team Premium at 50 percent limits within 48 hours of Kimi K3

The Decoder · 2026-07-19 · Source ↗

Anthropic announced Sunday evening that Claude Fable 5 will be permanently bundled into Max and Team Premium plans from Monday, July 20 at 50 percent of the previous usage limits. Pro and Team Standard revert to API-only access with a one-time $100 credit and metered pricing at $10 input and $50 output. The Decoder read the move as an acknowledgement that Kimi K3 broke the metered-only pricing case.

Anthropic announced the Claude Fable 5 pricing reversal on Sunday, July 19 at 20:04 UTC via its status page and CEO Dario Amodei's X account. Effective Monday, July 20, Fable 5 is permanently bundled into the Max and Team Premium subscription plans at 50 percent of the usage limits that Sonnet 5 and Opus 4.8 had previously carried. Metered-only access is retired for those tiers. Pro and Team Standard subscribers, the mid-tier group, lose plan-included access to Fable 5 entirely, revert to Sonnet 5 as their default model, and receive a one-time $100 credit toward Fable 5 API consumption at the standard $10 input and $50 output per million tokens.

The Decoder's coverage was the first to frame the announcement as a direct response to Kimi K3. Anthropic had held the position through June and early July that metered pricing was structural because Fable 5 was too expensive to serve inside a subscription-bundle price the market would accept. Kimi K3 broke the technical version of that argument by demonstrating that a Frontend Code Arena-leading model could be served at $3 input and $15 output, roughly 30 percent of Fable 5's price. Anthropic's reversal is a pricing capitulation on the enterprise seat side.

"We heard the feedback that metering the top-tier model out of the subscription plans was not the right call for our developer customers. The Kimi launch clarified the pricing math. Effective Monday, Fable 5 is included at half limits."— Dario Amodei, X post, 2026-07-19 20:04 UTC

The Pro-tier casualty

The Pro-tier segmentation is the interesting piece. Anthropic could have folded Fable 5 back into every tier at reduced limits. Instead it drew a line at Pro and Team Standard and shipped a one-time $100 credit as the compensation. Anthropic's internal telemetry, discussed in a follow-on Reddit AMA on Sunday night by Anthropic's product lead, shows that Pro-tier developer usage is concentrated on a small set of high-volume users whose consumption would push metered spend past the subscription price inside a week. The $100 credit is designed to soften the churn for those users while re-orienting Pro-tier as a Sonnet-5 subscription. The mid-tier developer buyer now has a genuine choice between Sonnet 5 at the Pro subscription price and Kimi K3 or Gemini 3.5 Pro on API-metered access at similar total cost.

The knock-on effect for Anthropic's enterprise sales cycle is the more consequential question. Team Premium at 50 percent Fable 5 limits works as a pricing anchor if customers view Fable 5 as the flagship. If Kimi K3's July 27 open-weights drop compresses the Fable 5 differentiation further, the 50 percent limits will read as a floor and the next pricing move for Anthropic will be into the enterprise seat rate directly.

Microsoft's July Patch Tuesday lands a record 570 CVEs, credits an AI-powered vulnerability-discovery pipeline

gHacks · 2026-07-15 · Source ↗

Microsoft's July Patch Tuesday shipped 570 CVEs, the largest single-month release on record, and credited an internal AI-powered vulnerability-discovery pipeline running against the Windows codebase for the surge. Three of the CVEs were zero-days already under active exploitation, including a SharePoint Server unauthenticated deserialization RCE that CISA added to the KEV catalog with a July 19 federal patch deadline.

Microsoft's July 2026 Patch Tuesday shipped on Tuesday, July 15 with 570 CVEs across the Windows, Office, SharePoint, Azure and Copilot product surfaces. The count is Microsoft's largest single-month release on record, roughly triple the trailing 12-month monthly average of 190. Microsoft's Security Response Center attributed the surge to an internal AI-powered vulnerability-discovery pipeline that has been running against the Windows and Office codebases since late 2025. The pipeline is not disclosed in detail but is described by Microsoft's blog post as an agentic system that reads source code, generates likely defect hypotheses, writes proof-of-concept exploits, and files internal bugs when a hypothesis is confirmed.

Three of the CVEs are zero-days under active exploitation prior to Patch Tuesday. The most consequential is CVE-2026-58644, a SharePoint Server unauthenticated deserialization remote-code-execution flaw at CVSS 9.8. CISA added the SharePoint CVE to the Known Exploited Vulnerabilities catalog on Wednesday, July 16 with a two-business-day federal patch deadline of Friday, July 19. The second zero-day is an Active Directory Federation Services privilege escalation at CVSS 8.8, with a working exploit observed in the wild targeting Fortune 500 domain controllers. The third is a BitLocker bypass at CVSS 7.6 that was publicly disclosed before the patch shipped.

"Half of the 570 CVEs in this month's release were discovered by our internal AI vulnerability-discovery pipeline. This is not an announcement of a new product. It is a disclosure that the tooling has matured enough to change our patch cadence."— Aanchal Gupta, VP, Microsoft Security Response Center
